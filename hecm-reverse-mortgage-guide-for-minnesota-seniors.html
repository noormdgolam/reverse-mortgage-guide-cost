HECM Reverse Mortgage Guide for Minnesota Seniors

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

A HECM Reverse Mortgage is a type of loan that allows Minnesota seniors to convert the equity in their home into tax-free funds, eliminating monthly mortgage payments while allowing them to maintain homeownership.

Eligibility for a HECM Reverse Mortgage is based on age, credit history, income, and home ownership, and typically requires the homeowner to be at least 62 years old. The home, whether it is a single-family home, townhouse, condominium, or a 2-4 unit property where the homeowner resides, must be the homeowner's primary residence.

The primary advantage of a HECM Reverse Mortgage is that it allows homeowners to maintain ownership of their property, access funds without monthly mortgage payments, and avoid the risk of foreclosure as long as the property taxes and homeowner's insurance are current. The loan also does not affect Social Security benefits.

However, it is essential for Minnesota seniors to understand the potential risks and costs associated with HECM Reverse Mortgages, which include:

  • Accumulated interest on the loan, which can result in a growing mortgage balance over time
  • Monthly property taxes and homeowner's insurance, which must be paid from the loan proceeds or other sources
  • Upfront fees for the originator and lender, which can range from 2-5% of the home's value
  • Potential reductions in Medicaid benefits due to an increase in home equity
  • Passing the loan balance to heirs or estate, if a fixed-rate loan is chosen or the borrower moves out of the home

To qualify for a HECM Reverse Mortgage, Minnesota seniors will need to complete a counseling session, usually conducted by a HUD-approved counseling agency, to ensure that they understand the terms and conditions of the loan. This counseling is mandatory under federal law and must be completed at any time before closing.

The loan amount that Minnesota seniors can borrow through a HECM Reverse Mortgage is based on a formula that takes into account the home's value, interest rates, and the borrower's age. The funds from the loan can be used for various purposes, including:

  • Paying off existing mortgage balance
  • Home repairs and maintenance
  • Home modifications to accommodate disabilities
  • Paying off credit cards
  • Travel or other personal expenses

It is essential for Minnesota seniors to carefully evaluate their financial situation, consider alternative sources of funds, and thoroughly review the terms and conditions of the loan before entering into a HECM Reverse Mortgage. Additionally, it is crucial to select a reputable and experienced lender to ensure the loan is properly originated and serviced.

In Minnesota, it is also essential for seniors to consider the Minnesota Reverse Mortgage Tax Credit, which allows seniors who are residents of Minnesota to claim a state tax credit of 20-30% of the origination fee of the loan. Homeowners should consult with a tax professional or financial advisor to determine their eligibility for this tax credit.
