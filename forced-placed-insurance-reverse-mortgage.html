Forced Placed Insurance: Why You Must Never Let It Happen

By Reverse Mortgage Guide Team Updated: April 06, 2024

  • You must provide proof of active homeowners insurance every year.
  • If your policy cancels, the lender will buy a policy for you at triple the cost.
  • Forced Placed Insurance only protects the lender, not your personal belongings.

Forced Placed Insurance: Why You Must Never Let It Happen | Reverse Mortgage Guide

A reverse mortgage is a type of mortgage that allows homeowners 62 years or older to borrow money using the equity in their home as collateral. While it can be a valuable tool for accessing funds, some lenders are known to use a shady tactic called forced placed insurance in an attempt to profit from the loan. In this article, we will delve into the world of forced placed insurance and explain why you must never let it happen when considering a reverse mortgage.

What is Forced Placed Insurance?

Forced placed insurance, also known as lender-placed insurance (LPI), is a type of insurance policy that lenders impose on homeowners when their standard homeowners insurance policy lapses or expires. While legitimate, LPI can become a problem when lenders abuse the practice and charge homeowners exorbitant premiums, often without their consent or knowledge.

In the context of reverse mortgages, forced placed insurance can be particularly egregious. Lenders may require homeowners to purchase force-placed insurance as a condition of the loan, and in some cases, the premiums for these policies can be astronomical, adding thousands of dollars to the homeowner's debt.

The Dangers of Forced Placed Insurance

Forced placed insurance can have several negative consequences for homeowners, particularly those who are already vulnerable financially. Some of the dangers of forced placed insurance include:

  • Exorbitant premiums: Homeowners may be charged high premiums for force-placed insurance, which can quickly accumulate and add to their debt.
  • Lack of transparency: Lenders may not disclose the costs of force-placed insurance or the terms of the policy to homeowners, making it difficult for them to understand and contest the charges.
  • Difficulty disputing claims: Homeowners may find it challenging to dispute claims related to force-placed insurance, particularly if they are not familiar with the terms of their reverse mortgage.
  • Increased debt: Forced placed insurance can increase homeowners' debt and put a strain on their financial resources, making it more difficult for them to pay their mortgage, property taxes, and other expenses.

Protecting Yourself from Forced Placed Insurance

Steps to Take

To protect yourself from forced placed insurance, follow these steps:

  • Read your loan documents carefully: Take the time to review your reverse mortgage agreement and understand the terms and conditions, including any requirements related to insurance.
  • Make payments on time: Paying your mortgage on time can help prevent lenders from imposing forced placed insurance.
  • Shop around for insurance: If you need to purchase homeowners insurance, shop around and compare rates to find the best deal.
  • Dispute questionable charges: If you notice charges for force-placed insurance on your account, dispute them immediately and ask the lender to explain the charges.

Seeking Help

If you are struggling to understand or dispute charges related to force-placed insurance, consider seeking help from a financial advisor, housing counselor, or attorney who specializes in reverse mortgages.

Conclusion

The risks associated with forced placed insurance can be significant, particularly for homeowners who are already vulnerable financially. By understanding the risks and taking steps to protect yourself, you can avoid the pitfalls of forced placed insurance and ensure that your reverse mortgage works for you, not against you.
