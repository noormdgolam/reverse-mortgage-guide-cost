HECM Reverse Mortgage Guide for Iowa Seniors

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Iowa seniors aged 62 and above can benefit from the HUD (U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development) Home Equity Conversion Mortgage (HECM) program, a reverse mortgage designed to help homeowners supplement their retirement income.

The HECM program is insured by the Federal Housing Administration (FHA), providing financial security for Iowa seniors who use their home equity to receive tax-free funds, pay off debts, or enhance their quality of life.

To qualify for the HECM program, homeowners must:

  • Be at least 62 years old (or the non-borrowing spouse in married households, regardless of age)
  • Own and reside in their primary residence, a one- to four-unit property, a condo, townhouse, or house
  • Possess a minimum amount of equity in their home (varies by location)
  • Have a valid government-issued ID
  • Pass a credit check and have no outstanding FHA liens

The HECM program comes with several benefits specifically designed for Iowa seniors:

  • Flexibility: Funds may be received in the form of a lump sum, monthly payments for life, or a line of credit
  • No monthly mortgage payments or property taxes and insurance for Iowa homeowners who choose the monthly payout option
  • Ability to defer payment of property taxes using a property tax deferral plan in Iowa (Iowa Code Chapter 428C)

The disbursement options available to Iowa seniors through the HECM program include:

  • Lump sum: Receive the loan proceeds upfront, which may be used for any purpose, such as paying off debts, covering living expenses, or financing home repairs
  • Line of credit: A revolving, interest-only line of credit that can be drawn upon at any time and repayable at any time
  • Combination: A combination of the above options

There are also some specific rules and considerations for non-borrowing spouses:

  • Non-borrowing spouses do not have a direct claim to the loan proceeds
  • If the borrower passes away, the non-borrowing spouse can remain in the home for their lifetime, or up to a maximum period determined by the loan's terms
  • The non-borrowing spouse must continue to pay property taxes and insurance and maintain the property

Before applying for the HECM program, it is essential for Iowa seniors to consult with a HUD-approved HECM counselor or a trusted financial advisor to discuss their specific situation and determine the best course of action.

Iowa seniors should also be aware of the potential drawbacks and costs associated with the HECM program, such as:

  • Accumulating interest over time, which can negatively impact the loan's balance
  • Mortgage insurance premiums (MIPs) added to the loan balance
  • Potential property tax and insurance increases, which can strain the homeowner's resources

The HECM program can be a powerful tool for Iowa seniors to unlock the equity in their homes and enjoy their retirement in financial stability. By understanding the program's benefits and rules, seniors can make informed decisions and maximize their financial options.
