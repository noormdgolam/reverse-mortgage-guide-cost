Reverse Mortgages for Veterans: Are There Special VA Benefits?

By Reverse Mortgage Guide Team Updated: May 06, 2024
𝕏 Share f Share in Share P Pin

Key Takeaways

  • The Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) does not offer or insure reverse mortgages.
  • Veterans must use the standard FHA HECM program like everyone else.
  • Predatory marketing often uses fake military logos to trick veterans into applying.

Millions of veterans successfully used their hard-earned VA loan benefits to purchase their homes with zero down payment. Now that they are retiring, many veterans naturally assume there is a "VA Reverse Mortgage" program available to help them access their equity.

There is no such thing as a VA Reverse Mortgage.

The Department of Veterans Affairs does not offer, guarantee, or insure reverse mortgages. If a veteran wants a reverse mortgage, they must use the exact same FHA-insured Home Equity Conversion Mortgage (HECM) program as civilian borrowers.

The Predatory Marketing Trap

Because veterans trust the VA, predatory lenders often use highly deceptive marketing tactics to trick veterans into applying for standard HECM loans.

They will send mailers covered in American flags, bald eagles, and official-looking crests. The envelopes often say "Important Notice Regarding Your Military Housing Benefits" or "Veteran Equity Release Program."

When the veteran calls the number, they are simply connected to a standard, civilian mortgage broker who sells them a high-fee FHA reverse mortgage.

How Veterans Can Actually Protect Themselves

If you are a veteran considering a reverse mortgage, you should approach the transaction exactly like a civilian: 1. Compare Margins: Do not blindly trust a lender because they put an eagle on their brochure. Get quotes from three different lenders and compare their interest rate margins and closing costs. 2. Check for Fee Waivers: Ask if the lender waives the origination fee for military veterans. Many reputable brokers will happily give veterans a massive discount on closing costs if asked. 3. Counseling: Ensure you take your mandatory HUD counseling session with an agency that understands veteran-specific issues, such as how reverse mortgage proceeds interact with VA disability payouts or Aid and Attendance benefits.
