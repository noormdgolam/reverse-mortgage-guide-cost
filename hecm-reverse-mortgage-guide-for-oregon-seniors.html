HECM Reverse Mortgage Guide for Oregon Seniors

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

The Home Equity Conversion Mortgage (HECM) is a type of reverse mortgage insured by the Federal Housing Administration (FHA) and available to Oregon seniors through the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD). The HECM reverse mortgage guide is designed to provide Oregon seniors with a comprehensive overview of the benefits, eligibility requirements, and application process for this financial solution.

Benefits of HECM Reverse Mortgage in Oregon:
The HECM reverse mortgage offers several benefits to Oregon seniors, including:

  • Increased cash-flow: An HECM reverse mortgage allows Oregon seniors to borrow a portion of their home's equity, freeing up their monthly income for living expenses, paying off debt, or funding long-term care.
  • Home equity access: This type of mortgage allows seniors to access their home's equity without selling their property or taking on additional debt.
  • No monthly mortgage payments: The borrower does not have to make monthly mortgage payments on an HECM.
  • Guaranteed home equity: The HECM insurance protects the borrower's right to remain in their home if repayments or property taxes are late.

Eligibility Requirements for HECM Reverse Mortgage in Oregon:
Oregon seniors interested in obtaining a HECM reverse mortgage must meet the following eligibility requirements:

  • Age requirement: The borrower must be at least 62 years old.
  • Creditworthiness: The borrower must have good credit history.
  • Property requirements: The home must be owner-occupied, meet FHA property standards, and be free of liens or other encumbrances.
  • Income requirements: The borrower's income must not exceed the applicable FHA limits.
  • Property value: The home's value must meet or exceed the applicable FHA limits.

HECM Reverse Mortgage Application Process in Oregon:
The HECM reverse mortgage application process in Oregon typically involves the following steps:

  • Home appraisal: An FHA-approved appraiser will visit the property to determine its value.
  • Counseling requirement: The borrower must participate in a counseling session with a HUD-approved counselor to understand the implications of the HECM reverse mortgage.
  • Application submission: The borrower must apply for the HECM reverse mortgage through a participating lender.
  • Underwriting and approval: The lender will review the application and ensure that it meets FHA requirements.

Additional Considerations for Oregon Seniors:
Oregon seniors considering a HECM reverse mortgage should also note that:

  • Compound interest rates: The borrower will pay compound interest on the outstanding loan balance.
  • Repayment requirements: The borrower or their estate must repay the HECM loan when the borrower dies, sells the property, or permanently moves out.
  • Taxes and insurance: The borrower is responsible for paying property taxes and insurance on the property.

Conclusion:
A HECM reverse mortgage can provide Oregon seniors with a valuable financial solution, allowing them to access their home's equity without incurring monthly mortgage payments. To ensure that this financial solution is right for you, consult with a financial advisor or a licensed mortgage professional to discuss your specific situation and options.
