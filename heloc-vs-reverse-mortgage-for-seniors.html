HELOC (Home Equity Line of Credit) vs. Reverse Mortgage

By Reverse Mortgage Guide Team Updated: March 01, 2024
𝕏 Share f Share in Share P Pin

Key Takeaways

  • HELOCs are cheap to set up but can be frozen or cancelled by the bank at any time.
  • HELOCs require monthly payments and fully reset after 10 years, causing a massive payment shock.
  • A HECM line of credit is guaranteed by the FHA and actually grows over time.

Financial advisors often tell seniors to get a Home Equity Line of Credit (HELOC) instead of a reverse mortgage because the closing costs are cheaper.

While it is true that a HELOC is cheaper to set up, using a HELOC as a long-term retirement safety net is one of the most dangerous financial mistakes a senior can make.

The Hidden Danger of the HELOC

A HELOC is essentially a massive credit card tied to your house. You only pay interest on the money you actually draw out.

1. The Bank Can Freeze It: HELOCs are not guaranteed. If the real estate market takes a slight downturn, or if your personal credit score drops, the bank has the legal right to instantly freeze or cancel your HELOC, cutting off your access to cash exactly when you need it most.

2. The 10-Year Reset Shock: The most devastating feature of a HELOC is the "Draw Period." For the first 10 years, you only have to make tiny interest-only payments. But at Year 11, the draw period ends. The loan enters the "Repayment Period." Your payment suddenly spikes to include full principal and interest, often tripling your monthly bill overnight. Thousands of seniors have lost their homes to foreclosure because they could not afford the Year 11 payment shock.

The Superiority of the HECM Line of Credit

The reverse mortgage line of credit was designed specifically to fix the flaws of the HELOC for the senior demographic.

1. It Cannot Be Frozen: Because a HECM is insured by the FHA, the lender cannot freeze or cancel your line of credit, even if the housing market crashes by 50%. Your access to your money is federally guaranteed.

2. No Monthly Payments: You never have to make a monthly payment on the HECM line of credit, eliminating the risk of a "payment shock" foreclosure in the future.

3. The Growth Rate: The most powerful feature of the HECM line of credit is that the unused portion actually grows over time. If you leave $50,000 in your credit line untouched, and the growth rate is 6%, next year you will have $53,000 available to borrow. If you live a long time, the credit line can grow to be larger than the actual value of your home.
