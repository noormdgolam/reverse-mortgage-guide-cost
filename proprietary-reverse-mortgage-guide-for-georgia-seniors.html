Proprietary Reverse Mortgage Guide for Georgia Seniors

As a senior citizen in Georgia, you may be eligible for a proprietary reverse mortgage, a type of loan that allows homeowners to borrow money using the equity in their home without making monthly mortgage payments.

A proprietary reverse mortgage is a non-government-backed loan offered by companies such as Financial Freedom or OneReverse Mortgage, typically with more stringent requirements and often more favorable terms than a HECM (Home Equity Conversion Mortgage) reverse mortgage.

To be eligible for a proprietary reverse mortgage in Georgia, you must be a homeowner who is at least 62 years old, occupy the home as your primary residence, and have sufficient equity built up in your home.

Pros and Cons of Proprietary Reverse Mortgages

  • Advantages:
  • No income or credit score requirements
  • No monthly mortgage payments are required, as long as you live in the home
  • Loan proceeds can be used for any purpose, such as home repairs, travel, or daily living expenses
  • Many proprietary reverse mortgages offer better interest rates and terms than HECMs
  • Disadvantages:
  • You must pay fees to access the loan proceeds, which can range from 2% to 6% of the loan amount, depending on the lender
  • Proprietary reverse mortgages often have stricter requirements for loan-to-value ratios, so you may not qualify if you have a smaller amount of equity in your home
  • You will still be responsible for paying property taxes, homeowner's insurance, and maintenance costs
  • You may not have the protection of the federal government, as HECMs are insured by HUD

How Proprietary Reverse Mortgages Work

Here's a step-by-step overview of the process:

  1. Apply for the loan with a participating lender, such as Financial Freedom
  2. The lender will evaluate your credit report, income (if applicable), and property value
  3. You will undergo a counseling session to discuss the terms of the loan and its implications
  4. Once approved, you can choose how to receive your loan proceeds, such as a lump sum, monthly installments, or a line of credit
  5. You will be required to complete a title search and an appraisal to determine the value of your home
  6. Once the loan is funded, you will not be required to make mortgage payments as long as you live in the home

Conclusion

Proprietary reverse mortgages can provide senior citizens in Georgia with access to much-needed funds to live comfortably in their own homes. However, it is essential to carefully consider the pros and cons and to consult with a housing counseling agency or a trusted financial advisor to determine if a proprietary reverse mortgage is the right choice for you.

Before applying for a proprietary reverse mortgage, review the terms and conditions carefully and ask plenty of questions to ensure you understand the implications of the loan. It's also crucial to keep in mind that proprietary reverse mortgages may have stricter requirements and higher fees than HECMs, so it's essential to compare rates and terms before making a decision.

By doing your research and working with a reliable lender, you can make an informed decision about whether a proprietary reverse mortgage is right for you, enabling you to maintain your independence and live comfortably in your Georgia home.
