Understanding Proprietary Reverse Mortgage Options in New Hampshire

As a senior resident of New Hampshire, you're likely aware of the various financial options available to you. If you're looking to tap into the equity built up in your home, a reverse mortgage may be the right choice. However, not all reverse mortgages are created equal, and proprietary reverse mortgages offer a unique set of benefits and requirements.

Proprietary reverse mortgages are offered by individual companies, unlike government-insured loans, which are backed by government agencies. This distinction gives companies the flexibility to tailor their products and services to meet the specific needs of their customers.

Benefits of Proprietary Reverse Mortgages in New Hampshire

  • Larger Loan Amounts**: Proprietary reverse mortgages often have higher loan limits than government-insured loans, allowing home owners to access more of their home's equity.
  • More Customization**: Proprietary lenders can offer loan terms and options tailored to the individual financial needs of borrowers, such as adjustable and fixed interest rates.
  • Less Burden on Home Value**: Some proprietary reverse mortgages have more flexible underwriting requirements, allowing home owners to access more of their home's equity without worrying about the impact on its value.

Requirements and Considerations

While proprietary reverse mortgages can offer greater flexibility, they may also come with stricter requirements and higher costs. Home owners should carefully consider the following:

  • Higher interest rates, which can result in higher loan balances and ongoing interest expenses.
  • Stricter eligibility requirements, which may include higher credit score minimums or income restrictions.
  • Shorter repayment terms or penalties for prepayment, which can reduce your ability to access future equity.

Examples of Proprietary Reverse Mortgages

SOME notable proprietary reverse mortgage companies include:

  • Home Equity Conversion Mortgage (HECM) alternatives, such as the H2Oh! reverse Mortgage that offer customized loan terms.
  • Private companies that offer more flexible loan options, such as the Nationwide Reverse Mortgage.

Evaluating Your Options in New Hampshire

  1. Consult with a reputable lender or financial advisor to assess your specific needs and explore proprietary reverse mortgage options.
  2. Carefully review the terms and conditions of any loan agreement before signing.
