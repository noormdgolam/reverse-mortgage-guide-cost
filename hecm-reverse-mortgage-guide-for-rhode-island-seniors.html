HECM Reverse Mortgage Guide for Rhode Island Seniors

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Understanding HECM Reverse Mortgage in Rhode Island

A Home Equity Conversion Mortgage (HECM) reverse mortgage is a federally insured loan program designed to help eligible homeowners in Rhode Island, and other states, utilize the equity in their property. Also known as a "reverse" mortgage, the HECM program allows homeowners aged 62 and above to borrow money based on the equity in their home without making monthly mortgage payments.

Facts About HECM Reverse Mortgage in Rhode Island

  • The primary borrower must be at least 62 years old to be eligible for a HECM reverse mortgage.
  • The borrower(s) must reside in the property for which the HECM is being taken.
  • The primary borrower must be a US citizen or a lawful permanent resident.
  • The property must be the primary residence of the borrower(s).
  • The property must be the borrower's main residence and not an investment property or a vacation home.
  • The HECM reverse mortgage is insured by the United States Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD).

How to Qualify for a HECM Reverse Mortgage in Rhode Island

Eligibility Requirements

The following requirements must be met to qualify for a HECM reverse mortgage:

  • The borrower(s) must have enough equity in their home to qualify.
  • The borrower(s) must own the property outright or have a low mortgage balance.
  • The borrower(s) must be financially eligible and unable to obtain credit elsewhere.
  • The borrower(s) must have a valid property deed in their name or their spouse's name.
  • The property must meet HUD's property value and condition requirements.

Types of HECM Reverse Mortgage Products in Rhode Island

Some of the most common types of HECM reverse mortgage products in Rhode Island include:

  • Traditional HECM Reverse Mortgage
  • Proprietary Reverse Mortgage (non-FHA insured)
  • Reverse Mortgage with an Adjustable Rate
  • Non-FHA Reverse Mortgage (proprietary products)

Benefits of HECM Reverse Mortgage in Rhode Island

A HECM reverse mortgage offers the following benefits:

  • No monthly mortgage payments required.
  • The ability to stay in one's home and maintain ownership.
  • The opportunity to receive tax-free cash, which may be used to supplement retirement income.
  • No risk to the lender, as the borrower is solely responsible for property maintenance and taxes.

Challenges and Considerations of HECM Reverse Mortgage in Rhode Island

Seniors in Rhode Island should consider the following:

  • Increased debt, with the loan balance increasing over time.
  • Potential impact on government benefits, such as Medicaid or Supplemental Security Income (SSI).
  • The possibility of losing the home if loan payments become delinquent.
  • The importance of consulting with a qualified HECM counselor or an attorney before making any decisions.

Conclusion

A HECM reverse mortgage is a flexible and valuable tool for eligible homeowners in Rhode Island who wish to utilize the equity in their property without making monthly mortgage payments. However, it is essential to carefully weigh the pros and cons of a HECM reverse mortgage before making any decisions.
