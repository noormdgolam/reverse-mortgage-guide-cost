Proprietary Reverse Mortgage Guide for Rhode Island Seniors

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Rhode Island seniors may be able to use the funds from a proprietary reverse mortgage for various home-related expenses, medical costs, or other essential needs, however, it is essential to choose the right product that suits individual circumstances.

Unlike government-insured Home Equity Conversion Mortgages (HECM), proprietary reverse mortgages are private products originated by specialized lenders. Since these products are not insured by government agencies, the qualification process and terms can be more complex, but also offer the potential for higher loan-to-value ratios.

Qualifying factors may include age, property ownership, and minimum equity requirements, but the key difference lies in the loan-to-value (LTV) ratio. HECM typically limits the LTV ration to 37% or 50% in some cases, while proprietary products can have LTV's exceeding 70% or in some cases up to 100%, depending on the program.

Additionally, to qualify for a proprietary reverse mortgage, homeowners will need to be 62 years old, be the sole owners of the property, and have sufficient equity in the home. The loan amount will depend on the value of the property, not just the homeowners' age.

Proprietary reverse mortgage lenders also offer various plans that allow for line-of-credit, single disbursement lump sum, monthly installments, term and tenure options. The plans may vary from lender to lender, and some options may be only available in combination with the line-of-credit or lump sum.

One major aspect to be acknowledged is the potential increase in interest rate due to a fixed or variable interest rate. It may lead to increased debt over time. A homeowner is only able to borrow up to a certain percentage of the home's value, and they will still be responsible for property taxes and insurance and the loan can be due if not repaid. In this case, the entire outstanding balance will be due.

Another significant benefit is that, unlike HECM mortgages, proprietary reverse mortgages may allow homeowners to keep their home's title, thus avoiding the need to transfer title to an heir or the lender. However, the loan terms and conditions should be carefully reviewed to understand the responsibilities and potential risks.

Rhode Island seniors can benefit from consulting with a licensed and experienced lender who specializes in reverse mortgage products to explore their options and discuss which proprietary plan is suitable based on their personal and financial needs.
