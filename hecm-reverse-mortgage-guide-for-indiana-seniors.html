HECM Reverse Mortgage Guide for Indiana Seniors

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

The Home Equity Conversion Mortgage (HECM) is a type of reverse mortgage designed for eligible Indiana seniors, offering a financial lifeline to help meet housing costs and improve cash flow in retirement. As a federally insured program managed by the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD), HECM loans provide unparalleled security and flexibility for homeowners aged 62 and older.

To qualify for an HECM loan in Indiana, applicants must meet specific requirements, including:

  • Satisfying age: 62 years or older
  • Ownership: The borrower must occupy the property as their primary residence.
  • Debt-to-Income Ratio: Low debt and sufficient income to fund ongoing expenses.
  • Property Value: The home's value must meet FHA's mortgage insurance requirements.

The HECM reverse mortgage process in Indiana involves the following critical steps:

1. Determining Eligibility and Counseling

The first step is to assess whether you're eligible for the loan and to complete a Housing and Urban Development-approved counseling session. This is mandatory before applying for an HECM loan, helping you and your family make informed decisions.

2. Selecting a Lender and Origination Fees

Select a certified lender who is a HUD-approved intermediary. Carefully evaluate the origination fees associated with the loan to ensure you understand the associated costs involved. The lender's origination fee typically ranges from 0.5 to 2% of the home's value but may vary depending on the selected loan program.

3. Property Value and Appraisal

The HECM lender will conduct an appraisal to determine the property's value and confirm it meets FHA's insurance requirements. The appraisal's purpose is to ensure you have sufficient equity in the property to support the loan's total amount borrowed.

4. Loan Amount and Disbursement Options

After determining the property's value, the lender will calculate the loan amount available to you. The available funds can be disbursed in a variety of ways, including:

  • Lump Sum: A single payment for a specified amount.
  • Monthly Payments
  • Line of Credit
  • Combination:

5. Reimbursement of Existing Mortgage

The existing mortgage on the property (if any) will be paid off using a portion of the borrowed HECM loan funds. This process is known as "payoff" or "reimbursement." The remaining funds, after repayment of existing debts, are then yours to enjoy or distribute according to your preference.

Understanding the intricacies of the HECM reverse mortgage in Indiana is crucial to ensure you reap the benefits responsibly. Consult a qualified professional to walk you through the process and determine if the HECM loan is suitable for your unique situation.
