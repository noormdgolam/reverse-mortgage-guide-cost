  • Proprietary reverse mortgage products allow homeowners to borrow a portion of their home's value without repaying the loan during their lifetime. This is often referred to as a "non-recourse" loan. However, upon the borrower's passing, the loan becomes due in full or the equity in the home is depleted based on the balance due.
  • These products are typically offered by private lenders, who often have more lenient qualification requirements. Some of these products may even consider credit scores and income as part of the underwriting process.
  • The interest rates associated with proprietary reverse mortgages can be higher than those offered by FHA-insured reverse mortgages. This is due to the lender's increased risk as they do not have the benefit of FHA insurance.
  • Homeowners in Massachusetts should note that there are potential tax implications associated with proprietary reverse mortgage products. Some loan products may be treated as taxable income when the homeowner receives the funds.
  • It is crucial for seniors in Massachusetts to carefully review and understand the terms and conditions of any proprietary reverse mortgage product before making a decision. It is highly recommended that seniors work with a licensed financial advisor or attorney to ensure that the product aligns with their individual goals and financial situation.

  • Benefits of proprietary reverse mortgage products in Massachusetts for seniors include:
    • Increased access to funds for individuals who may not qualify for FHA-insured reverse mortgage products.
    • More flexible qualification requirements compared to FHA-insured products.
    • Ability to maintain homeownership, as with FHA-insured products, but with the option to receive a lump sum or access funds via draw periods.

    • Limited availability compared to FHA-insured products.
    • Potentially higher interest rates.
