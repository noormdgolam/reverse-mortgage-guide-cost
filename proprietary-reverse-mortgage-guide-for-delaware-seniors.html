Proprietary Reverse Mortgage Guide for Delaware Seniors

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Introduction

In Delaware, seniors have a valuable resource known as a proprietary reverse mortgage, which can provide a steady stream of income, allowing them to stay in their homes and maintain a sense of independence. This article will serve as a comprehensive guide for Delaware seniors who are considering a proprietary reverse mortgage, helping them understand the benefits, the application process, and what to expect from the experience.

A proprietary reverse mortgage, also known as a jumbo reverse mortgage or a private reverse mortgage, is a type of reverse mortgage that is not insured by the Federal Housing Administration (FHA). While this means it does not come with the same benefits as an FHA-insured reverse mortgage, it also offers some advantages. For instance, proprietary reverse mortgages may have higher loan limits, allowing homeowners to access more of the equity in their homes.

Benefits of a Proprietary Reverse Mortgage

One of the primary benefits of a proprietary reverse mortgage is the opportunity to tap into a sizable amount of equity in your home. In Delaware, seniors can receive up to 45% of the equity in their homes, depending on the lender and the age of the borrower. This money can be used to cover essential expenses, pay off debt, or invest in other income-generating assets.

An additional benefit is the flexibility in payment options. Borrowers have the freedom to choose from various payment schedules, including a lump sum, monthly payments, or a line of credit. This allows them to tailor the repayment plan to their specific needs and financial goals.

Safety and security are also key advantages. Since the loan is secured by the home, borrowers are not required to repay the loan unless they choose to sell their property, move, or pass away. This means there is no risk of foreclosure and the senior can continue living in their home, worry-free.

The Application Process

The application process for a proprietary reverse mortgage is relatively straightforward, requiring the following documents and information:

  • Proof of ownership (title, deed, etc.)
  • Identification and Social Security number
  • Borrower's financial information (income, assets, credit report)
  • Home value and appraisal information (if necessary)
  • Counseling or educational requirements (if mandated by lender)

Once all the necessary documentation is in order, the lender will review the application and perform a thorough analysis of the home's value and the borrower's financial situation. If approved, the loan will be issued, and the borrower will receive the agreed-upon amount.

Things to Consider

Before applying for a proprietary reverse mortgage, seniors should be aware of a few things:

  • The interest rates may be higher compared to an FHA-insured reverse mortgage.
  • The loan limits are typically higher, but may also depend on the lender.
  • Appraisal requirements may be more stringent.
  • The lender's reputation and fees should be carefully scrutinized.

While a proprietary reverse mortgage can provide a valuable source of income for Delaware seniors, it is essential to weigh the pros and cons and understand the complexities involved. We recommend consulting with a financial advisor or an accredited reverse mortgage professional to make an informed decision.
