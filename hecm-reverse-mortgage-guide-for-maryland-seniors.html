HECM Reverse Mortgage Guide for Maryland Seniors

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

As a Maryland senior, you may be considering a Home Equity Conversion Mortgage (HECM) reverse mortgage as a way to supplement your retirement income or access the equity in your home. In this guide, we will outline the basics of HECM reverse mortgages, their benefits and risks, and provide information on how to qualify and apply for a HECM in Maryland.

A Comprehensive Overview of HECM Reverse Mortgages

A HECM reverse mortgage is a type of loan that allows homeowners to borrow money using the equity in their home as collateral. The loan is insured by the Federal Housing Administration (FHA) and is available to homeowners who are 62 years or older. The loan allows homeowners to receive tax-free funds and can be used to pay off debts, cover living expenses, or fund home repairs.

Key Benefits of HECM Reverse Mortgages

  • Access to tax-free cash: HECM reverse mortgages allow homeowners to receive tax-free funds, which can help supplement their retirement income.
  • No monthly mortgage payments: With a HECM reverse mortgage, homeowners are not responsible for making monthly mortgage payments.
  • No debt obligations: Once a HECM is finalized, the borrower and their non-borrowing spouse are not responsible for paying debt on the loan.
  • Cash flow flexibility: HECM reverse mortgages can provide homeowners with a steady cash flow, which can help them cover living expenses and other financial needs.

Risks and Considerations

While HECM reverse mortgages offer many benefits, they also come with some risks and considerations. These include:

  • Accumulating interest: With a HECM reverse mortgage, interest accrues on the loan, which can result in a larger balance over time.
  • Loss of equity: If the borrower passes away or sells the home, the borrower or their heirs will be responsible for repaying the loan balance, which can result in a loss of equity.
  • Maintenance and insurance responsibilities: Homeowners are still responsible for paying property taxes, insurance, and maintenance on the home.

Qualifying for a HECM Reverse Mortgage in Maryland

To qualify for a HECM reverse mortgage in Maryland, homeowners must meet certain requirements, including:

  • Age: Borrowers must be 62 years or older.
  • Home ownership: Borrowers must own their home and have paid off their mortgage.

Homeowners who are interested in a HECM should consult with a reverse mortgage specialist to discuss their options and determine whether a HECM is right for them.

Applying for a HECM Reverse Mortgage in Maryland

Homeowners who are interested in a HECM can begin the application process by:

  • Reaching out to a reverse mortgage specialist: Borrowers can contact a reverse mortgage lender or financial advisor who is certified to work with reverse mortgages.
  • Receiving counseling: Borrowers must receive counseling regarding the HECM prior to applying.
  • Completing the application: Borrowers will need to complete the application and provide documentation, including identification and proof of homeownership.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the cost of a HECM reverse mortgage?

A: The cost of a HECM reverse mortgage includes origination fees, closing costs, and interest accrued on the loan.

Q: Can I still qualify for a HECM if my spouse is younger than 62?

A: Borrowers must be 62 years or older to qualify for a HECM. Non-borrowing spouses, however, may qualify for a HECM, but their age will not affect the borrowing spouse's ability to qualify.

Q: Will a HECM affect my Medicaid benefits?

A: Borrowers should consult with a Medicaid representative to discuss how a HECM may affect their benefits. Generally, a HECM will not affect Medicaid benefits, but it may impact eligibility for home care services.
