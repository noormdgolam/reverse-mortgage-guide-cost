Proprietary Reverse Mortgage Guide for New York Seniors

A proprietary reverse mortgage is a type of home equity conversion mortgage (HECM) that is offered by private lenders, rather than the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD). As a New York senior, understanding the specifics of proprietary reverse mortgages is crucial in making an informed decision about your financial well-being.

Key Benefits and Features

  • Flexibility in payment options: Proprietary reverse mortgages offer a range of payment options, including lump sums, lines of credit, term payments, and a combination of these.
  • No requirement to purchase mortgage insurance: Unlike HECM loans, proprietary reverse mortgages do not require mortgage insurance, which can be a significant benefit for borrowers.
  • No limit on the amount borrowed: As long as you have sufficient equity in your home, you can borrow against the value of your property.
  • No restrictions on the use of funds: Proprietary reverse mortgages do not place restrictions on how you use the borrowed funds, whether for home repairs, living expenses, or any other purpose.

Credentials and Lender Requirements

Proprietary reverse mortgage lenders must be licensed with the New York Department of Financial Services and be registered with the National Mortgage Licensing System (NMLS).

Lenders must also provide a minimum level of disclosure and counseling to borrowers, ensuring that they fully understand the terms and conditions of the loan.

Evaluation Requirements

Proprietary reverse mortgage applicants must undergo an appraisal to determine the value of their home.

Lenders also typically require credit history, income, and debt information.

Borrowers must also be at least 62 years of age and occupy the home as their principal residence.

Closing Costs and Fees

The costs associated with a proprietary reverse mortgage in New York may include an origination fee, appraisal fee, title insurance fee, and other third-party charges.

Lenders may roll these costs into the loan amount or require upfront payment.

Borrowers should carefully review the terms of the loan and factor in the total costs of the loan when determining how much to borrow.

Government Involvement

While HUD does guarantee HECM loans, it does not participate in proprietary reverse mortgages.

However, some proprietary reverse mortgage lenders may offer special incentives or discounts for borrowers who meet specific criteria, such as low-income seniors or those who purchase energy-efficient home improvements.

Suggestions for New York Seniors

Before pursuing a proprietary reverse mortgage, New York seniors are encouraged to:

  • Consult with a reverse mortgage expert to determine whether a proprietary reverse mortgage is right for you.
  • Compare offers from multiple lenders to ensure you secure the best terms.
  • Thoroughly review the terms of the loan and factor in closing costs and fees.
  • Consider seeking advice from a financial advisor or attorney to ensure you make an informed decision.
