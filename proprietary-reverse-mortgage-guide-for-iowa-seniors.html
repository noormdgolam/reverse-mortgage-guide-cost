Proprietary Reverse Mortgage Guide for Iowa Seniors

A proprietary reverse mortgage, also known as a jumbo reverse mortgage, is a non-government insured reverse mortgage product offered by private lenders, often with more stringent qualification requirements, larger loan amounts and flexible payout options. As a senior citizen residing in Iowa, understanding the specifics of proprietary reverse mortgages is crucial when considering your financial options.

The majority of Iowa seniors utilizing reverse mortgages have been homeowners for more than a decade and reside in a single-family dwelling, a condominium or a townhouse. Eligibility for a proprietary reverse mortgage primarily depends on the borrower's age, home value and equity position. It is essential to note that there are no income verification or credit score requirements for proprietary reverse mortgages, making them an attractive option for retirees relying on fixed-income.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Borrowers must be at least 62 years of age
  • Primary residence must be owner-occupied
  • The borrower must hold title to the property in their name
  • The home must be located within the United States and meet the Uniform Standards of Professional Appraisal Practice (USPAP) standards
  • The borrower must reside in the home as their primary residence
  • Courts may consider factors when deciding whether someone resides 'in' a property

Loan Options

There are three primary ways to receive the funds from a proprietary reverse mortgage: a lump sum, a monthly payment or a line of credit. Borrowers can elect one, two or all of these options, which allows for customization of their funding.

  • Lump Sum: Received as a single payment at the time of closing.
  • Monthly Payment: Paid on a scheduled basis such as monthly or quarterly.
  • Line of Credit: A revolving line of credit that allows homeowners to borrow funds in an as-needed manner and repay with no interest accrued during non-borrowing periods.

Borrowers should also be aware that proprietary reverse mortgages may have more flexible repayment terms, including payment plans that may extend beyond the borrower's lifetime.

Interest Rates and Fees

Interest rates on proprietary reverse mortgages may be negotiable based on the lender, loan amount, and home value, and typically range from 7% to 9%. Fees can be steep as well, adding hundreds or thousands of dollars in closing and servicing fees.

However, many lenders offer proprietary reverse mortgages with a fixed interest rate for a specific number of years, which can provide the borrower with greater certainty in their cash flows. This fixed-rate option is often available if borrowers are willing to take out a larger loan for a shorter term.

Protecting Ownership

Proprietary reverse mortgages do not require repayment as long as the borrower occupies the property as their primary residence, provided they adhere to the terms and conditions of the loan. When the property is sold, refinanced, or otherwise no longer used as the borrower's primary residence, the borrower may be required to repay the outstanding loan balance.

Funding from a proprietary reverse mortgage does not affect the borrower's Social Security benefits or Medicaid eligibility. Furthermore, the loan cannot be declared unaffordable based solely on the monthly payments.

Next Steps

A proprietary reverse mortgage can provide a valuable source of additional income to Iowa seniors, especially those nearing retirement. As with any financial decision, however, a meeting with a qualified reverse mortgage professional or advisor is a prerequisite. They can help homeowners evaluate whether a proprietary reverse mortgage makes sense and assist in selecting the best loan option to suit individual financial needs.
