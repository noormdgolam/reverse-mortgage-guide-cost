Key Takeaways

  • You can use a POA to get a reverse mortgage for a parent, but the POA must meet strict HUD guidelines.
  • The POA must specifically grant the authority to encumber real estate.
  • The POA must have been signed while the parent still had mental competency.

A reverse mortgage is not a one-size-fits-all solution. Depending on your home's value, your age, and local property laws, the benefits can vary drastically. Let's explore exactly what you need to know.

\n\nOften, adult children realize a reverse mortgage is the only way to afford in-home care for a parent suffering from advanced dementia or Alzheimer's. Because the parent can no longer understand complex financial documents, the adult child must use a Power of Attorney (POA) to sign the reverse mortgage application on their behalf.

This is entirely legal, but reverse mortgage lenders scrutinize POA documents with extreme prejudice to prevent elder abuse.

The Competency Requirement

The most critical rule is timing. For a POA to be valid for a reverse mortgage, the document must have been signed by the parent when they were still legally and mentally competent.

If a doctor diagnosed your mother with severe dementia in 2020, and you had her sign a POA document in 2022, the lender's legal department will reject it instantly. You cannot force a cognitively impaired person to sign away the rights to their home. (In this tragic scenario, you would have to go to court and obtain formal Conservatorship or Guardianship, which is an expensive and lengthy process).

The Specific Powers Required

You cannot use a generic "Medical Power of Attorney" or a basic boilerplate POA downloaded from the internet.

The POA document must be a Durable Power of Attorney, meaning it remains in effect even if the principal becomes incapacitated. Furthermore, it must explicitly grant you the authority to: 1. Conduct real estate transactions. 2. Borrow money and encumber real estate with a mortgage lien.

If the document does not specifically mention the power to mortgage real estate, the title company will not allow the loan to close.

The HUD Counseling Hurdle

Remember that independent HUD counseling is mandatory. If you are acting as the POA, you must attend the counseling session on behalf of your parent.

The counselor will interview you to ensure that the reverse mortgage proceeds are being used strictly for the benefit of the parent (e.g., paying for their 24/7 nursing care), and not simply being pocketed by the adult child.

If you foresee your parents needing expensive care in the future, it is vital to have them execute a comprehensive Durable Power of Attorney while their minds are still sharp.\n

Crucial Eligibility Factors

  • Age Requirement: You typically must be at least 62 years old to qualify.
  • Home Equity: You must own your home outright or have a significant amount of equity built up.
  • Property Taxes & Insurance: Borrowers are still entirely responsible for ongoing property taxes, homeowners insurance, and general maintenance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can the bank take my home?

No. A reverse mortgage is a non-recourse loan. The lender cannot force you out of your home as long as you continue to pay property taxes, insurance, and maintain the home.
