Proprietary Reverse Mortgage Guide for Connecticut Seniors

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

As a Connecticut senior, understanding the options for leveraging your home's value can be a daunting task. With the rise of proprietary reverse mortgages, homeowners are now able to access funds from the equity in their homes without having to sell or relocate. In this guide, we will delve into the intricacies of proprietary reverse mortgages, examining the benefits and drawbacks of this financial tool.

In Connecticut, homeowners 62 years and older are eligible to apply for a proprietary reverse mortgage. This loan allows homeowners to tap into their home's equity, using the funds to cover living expenses, pay off outstanding debts, or finance home improvements. Unlike the more common Home Equity Conversion Mortgage (HECM), proprietary reverse mortgages do not require a Federal Housing Administration (FHA) guarantee, and thus offer more flexibility and customization options.

Proprietary reverse mortgages are private loans offered by lenders, and the regulations governing these products can vary significantly from state to state. Connecticut, for instance, has specific regulations regarding loan-to-value ratios, interest rates, and repayment terms. It's essential for seniors to consult with a qualified loan officer who is familiar with the state's laws and regulations.

The primary benefits of proprietary reverse mortgages include:

  • Improved cash flow: homeowners can access lump sums of cash, set up a monthly annuity, or establish a line of credit, providing them with financial flexibility.
  • Possibility of tax-free cash: under the Modified Accelerated Cost Recovery System (MACRS), homeowners may be able to claim tax deductions for the interest paid on their reverse mortgage.
  • No debt repayment: homeowners do not have to repay the loan until they permanently relocate from their home or until the property is no longer their primary residence.

However, there are also several drawbacks to consider:

  • Interest rates: proprietary reverse mortgages often come with interest rates higher than those associated with HECM loans.
  • High upfront fees: homeowners may be required to pay origination fees, closing costs, and other expenses, which can be substantial.
  • Risk of foreclosure: failure to meet repayment terms or other loan obligations can result in foreclosure, with the borrower facing significant financial losses.

To be eligible for a proprietary reverse mortgage, homeowners typically must meet the following requirements:

  • Age: be at least 62 years old.
  • Property ownership: own the home free and clear, or owe little to no balance on the primary mortgage.
  • Income: have a reliable source of income to cover basic living expenses.

Before making a decision, Connecticut seniors should carefully weigh the pros and cons of proprietary reverse mortgages, taking into account their personal financial circumstances and goals. It's crucial to consult with a qualified financial advisor or reverse mortgage expert to ensure that the chosen loan program aligns with their needs and objectives.

Connecticut's Department of Banking regulates the reverse mortgage industry and provides information and resources for homeowners.
