Proprietary Reverse Mortgage Guide for California Seniors

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Understanding California's Reverse Mortgage Laws

The proprietary reverse mortgage is a non-government-backed loan option available to California homeowners aged 62 and older. It allows homeowners to borrow money using their home's equity, which is not repaid until the homeowner passes away, sells the home, or permanently moves out. This comprehensive guide will provide an in-depth understanding of proprietary reverse mortgage options for California seniors.

Eligibility Requirements

To be eligible for a proprietary reverse mortgage, California homeowners must meet the following requirements:
  • Age:** The homeowner must be at least 62 years old.
  • Property Type:** The property must be a primary residence, such as a single-family home, condominium, or townhouse.
  • Property Equity:** The homeowner must own a sufficient amount of equity in their home to borrow against.
  • Debt-to-Value:** The property must not have excessive debt or liens that could negatively impact the loan.

Different Types of Proprietary Reverse Mortgage Options

California seniors have access to various proprietary reverse mortgage options, each with distinct features and benefits:
  • Home Equity Conversion Mortgage (HECM) Alternative:** A proprietary reverse mortgage is a non-HECM, meaning it is not insured by the federal government.
  • Single-Purpose Loans:** Limited to a specific purpose, such as home repairs or property taxes.
  • Interest-Only Loans:** Allows homeowners to withdraw interest payments without having to repay the principal amount.
  • Term Loans:** Provides a lump-sum payment over a fixed period or on a specified date.
  • Line-of-Credit Loans:** Allows homeowners to borrow money as needed, with no set repayment schedule.

Risk and Responsibilities

While a proprietary reverse mortgage can provide financial benefits, it also entails risks and responsibilities:
  • Market Risk:** The value of the home may depreciate over time, affecting the loan balance.
  • Repayment Risk:** When the homeowner passes away, sells the home, or permanently moves out, the lender must be repaid, including any accumulated interest.
  • Home Maintenance:** Homeowners are responsible for maintaining their property, as the lender will not cover repairs or upkeep.

Alternatives to Proprietary Reverse Mortgages

Understanding Reverse Mortgage Alternatives in California

Before pursuing a proprietary reverse mortgage, California seniors may want to consider alternative financial options:
  • Savings or Investments:** Utilizing existing savings or investments to cover expenses.
  • Government Benefits:** Maximizing available government benefits, such as Social Security or Medi-Cal.
  • Home Equity Loan or Line of Credit:** Exploring other types of traditional home equity loans with more stringent qualification criteria.
  • Home Reversion Agreements:** Selling a percentage of the home's equity to a third-party investor.
