HECM Reverse Mortgage Guide for Massachusetts Seniors

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

A Home Equity Conversion Mortgage (HECM) reverse mortgage is a federal government insured loan that allows Massachusetts seniors to convert a portion of their home's equity into tax-free cash.

Eligible borrowers must be at least 62 years old, occupying the property as their primary residence, and have a significant amount of equity in the home.

To qualify for a HECM reverse mortgage in Massachusetts, senior homeowners must meet the Department of Housing and Urban Development's (HUD) financial assessment requirements, which include:

  • Paying property taxes and insurance on time.
  • Maintaining a good credit history.
  • Having a stable income stream, either through social security or another pension plan.

Massachusetts senior homeowners can use their HECM reverse mortgage loan proceeds to cover living expenses, pay off existing mortgages, or finance home renovations and repairs.

The amount of loan proceeds available will depend on the borrower's age, the value of the property, and current interest rates. On average, Massachusetts seniors can borrow around 38-65% of their home's value, with maximum limits ranging from $765,600 to $970,800.

A HECM reverse mortgage requires no monthly mortgage payments, eliminating the need for a budget to cover housing expenses. However, borrowers must still pay property taxes on the home, as well as any homeowners insurance and maintenance costs.

Borrowers also have the option to use a HECM reverse mortgage to set up a line of credit or a series of monthly payments called the Modified Tenure Payment Plan, which can provide a predictable income stream over time.

The HECM reverse mortgage loan does not require a balance to be repaid until the home is sold, transferred, or the borrower passes away. This means that Massachusetts seniors do not have to worry about making monthly mortgage payments for the remainder of their lives.

It is worth noting that if the borrower is not the sole owner of the property, at least one occupant must meet the HECM eligibility requirements and occupy the property as a principal residence.

A HECM reverse mortgage can be a valuable tool for Massachusetts seniors who wish to stay in their homes while managing their housing expenses. However, borrowers should carefully review their financial situation and weigh the pros and cons before making a decision.

When considering a HECM reverse mortgage, it is essential that Massachusetts seniors consult with a qualified, HUD-approved reverse mortgage counselor or a reputable financial advisor to determine the best course of action.

Borrowers should also review the loan documents, which include the HECM counseling certificate, the loan application, and the closing disclosures, carefully before signing any agreements.

Additionally, Massachusetts seniors should be aware that a HECM reverse mortgage can have tax implications, such as reducing their Medicaid and Veterans Administration benefits due to increased home equity value, which may change after the loan is secured. They should discuss these implications with their financial advisor prior to applying for the loan.

Overall, a HECM reverse mortgage can provide Massachusetts seniors with a source of income to support their living expenses while maintaining the comfort and security of their home.

However, as with any financial decision, careful consideration and professional guidance are essential to ensure the best possible outcomes.
