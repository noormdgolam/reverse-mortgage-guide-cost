Proprietary Reverse Mortgage Guide for Tennessee Seniors

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

In the state of Tennessee, seniors have access to a range of financial solutions to help them maintain their quality of life, even in retirement. One option to consider is the proprietary reverse mortgage, a specialized type of reverse mortgage product designed for homeowners aged 62 and older.

A proprietary reverse mortgage is a non-government-backed mortgage that allows homeowners to borrow a portion of their home's value while still retaining ownership. With no monthly mortgage payments due, borrowers can tap into their home's equity to cover expenses such as taxes, homeowner's insurance, property maintenance, healthcare costs, and living expenses.

Proprietary reverse mortgage lenders offer a range of features and benefits to Tennessee seniors, including:

  • Higher loan-to-value (LTV) ratios: Proprietary reverse mortgages often offer higher LTV ratios than government-backed reverse mortgages, allowing borrowers to access a larger portion of their home's value.
  • Customized loan structures: Lenders can work with borrowers to create customized loan structures that meet their individual needs and financial goals.
  • No income or credit score requirements: Proprietary reverse mortgages do not require borrowers to demonstrate a minimum income or credit score, making them an attractive option for seniors with limited income or less-than-perfect credit.
  • No monthly mortgage payments: Borrowers are not required to make monthly mortgage payments, reducing their financial burden and increasing their cash flow.
  • Home maintenance and repair funding: Proprietary reverse mortgage lenders may provide funding for home maintenance and repair costs, helping to maintain the borrower's home and prevent costly repairs.

Key benefits for Tennessee seniors include:

  • Flexibility: Proprietary reverse mortgages offer flexible loan structures and repayment options that can be tailored to meet the borrower's individual needs.
  • Increased cash flow: By not requiring monthly mortgage payments, borrowers can enjoy increased cash flow and financial flexibility.
  • Home retention: Proprietary reverse mortgages allow borrowers to retain ownership of their home, providing a sense of security and stability.
  • No debt obligations: Borrowers are not required to take on debt or make monthly mortgage payments, reducing their financial burden.

It is essential for Tennessee seniors to carefully evaluate their financial options and consider working with a qualified mortgage professional to determine whether a proprietary reverse mortgage is the right choice for their individual circumstances.

Key considerations when choosing a proprietary reverse mortgage lender include:

  • Lender reputation: Research the lender's reputation, experience, and customer service record.
  • Loan terms and conditions: Carefully review the loan terms and conditions, including the interest rate, loan fees, and repayment requirements.
  • Creditworthiness: Understand the lender's creditworthiness guidelines and how they may impact the borrower's ability to secure a loan.
  • Loan-to-value ratio: Determine the loan-to-value ratio and how it may impact the borrower's available loan amount.

By carefully evaluating their options and working with a qualified mortgage professional, Tennessee seniors can enjoy the benefits of a proprietary reverse mortgage and maintain their quality of life in retirement.
