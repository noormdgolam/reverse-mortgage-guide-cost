Life Settlement: Selling Your Life Insurance Instead of Your Home

By Reverse Mortgage Guide Team Updated: March 15, 2024
𝕏 Share f Share in Share P Pin

Key Takeaways

  • A life settlement allows you to sell an unneeded life insurance policy to an investor.
  • You receive a massive lump sum of cash today, completely tax-free.
  • It is often a better choice than a reverse mortgage if you no longer need to protect young dependents.

Life Settlement: Selling Your Life Insurance Instead of Your Home

In the realm of reverse mortgages, it's easy to get overwhelmed by the numerous options and complexities involved. However, one lesser-known alternative deserves attention - the life settlement. This financial strategy allows you to sell your life insurance policy, leveraging the cash value to alleviate financial burdens or achieve your long-term goals.

Similar to a reverse mortgage, a life settlement is an agreement between you and a third-party investor who purchases the rights to your life insurance policy. This investor assumes liability for future premiums and will receive the policy's death benefit when you pass away. As the policyholder, you receive a lump-sum payment, which can be tax-free, depending on the policy type and investment strategy.

Key Benefits

  • Fund long-term care expenses without compromising assets
  • Supplement retirement income without tapping into qualified retirement accounts (QRAs)
  • Earn interest on cash value or surrender benefits
  • Relieve heirs from inherited policy debt or taxes

Types of Life Insurance Policies Eligible for Sale

Most permanent life insurance policies, such as:

  • Whole life insurance
  • Variable universal life insurance
  • Indexed universal life insurance
  • Variable life insurance
  • Survivorship life insurance

However, term life insurance policies and group life insurance policies are generally not considered viable for sale. Furthermore, insurance companies may reject policies with certain conditions or restrictions.

Qualifying for a Life Settlement

Typical requirements include:

  • Age 65 or older, but this may vary by state
  • Policy cash value and face value must be substantial (varies by state)
  • Policyholder and beneficiaries must agree to the sale

Life Insurance Appraisal

When selling a life insurance policy, you'll typically need to undergo an independent appraisal. This process assesses the policy's present value and determines its fair market worth. You'll be presented with the appraiser's report to guide your life settlement decision.

Comparison to Reverse Mortgages

While both alternatives involve tapping into policy cash value, the primary differences are:

  • Timeframe: Reverse mortgages generally take place in the later stages of retirement, whereas life settlements can occur at any age, depending on the policy terms.
  • Average payout: Reverse mortgage proceeds are typically smaller compared to life settlement payouts, averaging around $200,000.
  • Funding goals: Reverse mortgages aim to secure retirement expenses, whereas life settlements aim to supplement income or relieve inherited taxes.

By exploring life settlements as a complement to reverse mortgages, you can unlock new paths to securing your financial future. Remember, it's essential to discuss your circumstances with a qualified insurance expert or financial advisor to ensure the best fit for your specific situation.
