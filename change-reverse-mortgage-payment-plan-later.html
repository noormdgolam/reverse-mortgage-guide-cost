Can You Change Your Reverse Mortgage Payment Plan Later?

By Reverse Mortgage Guide Team Updated: April 16, 2024

Key Takeaways

  • FHA rules allow you to change your payout plan as often as you want.
  • You can convert an unused Line of Credit into guaranteed monthly income.
  • The servicer usually charges a tiny $20 administrative fee to make the change.

Can You Change Your Reverse Mortgage Payment Plan Later? | Reverse Mortgage Guide

A reverse mortgage is a complex financial instrument that can be tailored to an individual's needs, but also comes with a lot of restrictions and obligations. One of the key aspects of a reverse mortgage is the payment plan, which determines how the borrower will receive their funds. While a reverse mortgage provides financial security in retirement, changes in personal circumstances or market conditions may necessitate a change in the payment plan. But is it possible to change a reverse mortgage payment plan later?

In most cases, reverse mortgage payment plans are fixed and non-modifiable. Once chosen, the borrower cannot alter the payment structure or convert between different payment types. The three main payment options for a reverse mortgage are:

  • Monthly payment: A regular monthly payment is made to the borrower, often for a fixed term or for as long as they live in the property.
  • Line of credit: A line of credit is established, allowing the borrower to borrow and repay loan proceeds as needed.
  • Lump sum payment: A single lump sum payment is made to the borrower, who can use it as needed.

However, some reverse mortgage products may offer adjustable payment plans or allow borrowers to convert between payment types under certain conditions. For instance, if the borrower takes out a hybrid reverse mortgage, which combines a lump sum payment with a monthly payment option, they may be able to change their payment plan in the future. It's essential to discuss the payment plan options with a reverse mortgage professional or financial advisor to understand which products are available and which may be subject to change.

To change a reverse mortgage payment plan, borrowers may need to refinance the loan, which involves taking out a new loan product with different terms. Refinancing can be a useful option if the borrower wants to change their payment plan or access more funds. However, refinancing may come with additional fees, closing costs, and interest rates that may not be as favorable as the original loan. Additionally, borrowers may need to satisfy certain conditions, such as making a significant amount of payments or meeting specific financial requirements, to be eligible for a modified payment plan.

Before changing a reverse mortgage payment plan, borrowers should carefully consider their financial situation and the potential risks and benefits of making changes. It's crucial to seek the advice of a reverse mortgage professional or financial advisor to determine the best course of action and ensure they remain in compliance with federal regulations. They can help borrowers weigh the pros and cons of changing their payment plan, assess their financial situation, and identify alternative loan options that may better meet their needs.

Changes to reverse mortgage payment plans may also impact the overall cost of the loan, potentially increasing the borrower's total debt or extending the loan term. Borrowers should also be aware that changing a reverse mortgage payment plan may affect their eligibility for Medicaid, VA benefits, or other government assistance programs that rely on property values or income levels.

Ultimately, the ability to change a reverse mortgage payment plan later depends on the specific loan product, federal regulations, and individual circumstances. Borrowers should carefully evaluate their options and seek professional advice before making any changes to their reverse mortgage payment plan.
