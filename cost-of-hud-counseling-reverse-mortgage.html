The Cost of Independent HUD Counseling (and Fee Waivers)

By Reverse Mortgage Guide Team Updated: February 14, 2024
𝕏 Share f Share in Share P Pin

Key Takeaways

  • HUD counseling is a strict federal requirement for all HECM applicants.
  • The session typically costs between $125 and $200.
  • Counseling agencies are required to offer fee waivers to seniors experiencing severe financial hardship.

The Cost of Independent HUD Counseling and Fee Waivers | Reverse Mortgage Guide

When considering a reverse mortgage, it's crucial to understand the costs associated with the process, including independent Housing and Urban Development (HUD) counseling. This comprehensive guide will outline the costs and discuss potential fee waivers.

Independent HUD Counseling Costs

Independent HUD counseling is required to receive a reverse mortgage. These non-profit counseling organizations offer guidance on the reverse mortgage loan process and help borrowers avoid fraudulent activities. There are approximately 150 approved counseling agencies for reverse mortgages. The cost of HUD counseling varies between agencies and may range from $125 to $200.

However, if you're at least 62 years old and plan to use the reverse mortgage loan proceeds to live in the dwelling as your primary residence, you may be eligible for a fee waiver. Under the National Council on Aging (NCOA) and the National Foundation for Credit Counseling (NFCC) program, some agencies may waive their counseling fees. If you're unable to afford or don't want to pay the fee, it's recommended to reach out to these organizations directly.

Fee Waivers for Independent HUD Counseling

You may be eligible for a fee waiver if you meet the following criteria:
  • Low-income households: Some HUD-approved counseling agencies provide fee waivers for low-income households, those at or below 150% of the Federal Poverty Guidelines
  • Veterans and disabled individuals: If you're a veteran or disabled, you may be eligible for a fee waiver

To qualify for a fee waiver, you'll typically need to submit documentation verifying your income levels, age, or disability status. Some agencies offer online counseling sessions at a lower cost, which may help reduce the financial burden.

Additional Reverse Mortgage Costs

When considering a reverse mortgage loan, it's essential to be aware of additional fees associated with the process:
  • Origination fees: A fee charged by the lender when originating the loan
  • Mortgage insurance premium (MIP): An upfront fee paid by the borrower, which can range from 0.5% to 2% of the loan
  • Servicing fees: An ongoing fee paid by the borrower to the lender throughout the life of the loan
  • Setup fees: Costs associated with setting up the loan, such as document preparation and underwriting

When shopping for a reverse mortgage, it's recommended to consult with a lender who offers a low or no origination fee. These fees should be clearly disclosed in the loan estimate and may impact the overall cost of the loan.

Conclusion

When considering a reverse mortgage, it's crucial to understand the costs associated with independent HUD counseling and potential fee waivers. By exploring these options, you can make an informed decision and avoid unnecessary expenses. Be sure to research available counseling agencies and their fee waiver policies to minimize your financial burden.
