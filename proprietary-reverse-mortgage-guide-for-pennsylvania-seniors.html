Proprietary Reverse Mortgage Guide for Pennsylvania Seniors

Pennsylvania seniors have access to reverse mortgage options that may provide financial relief and assistance in paying for home renovations, healthcare expenses, or other living costs. A proprietary reverse mortgage, also known as a private reverse mortgage, is a non-government insured reverse mortgage product offered by lenders.

Eligibility Requirements

  • The borrower must be aged 62 years or older.
  • The home being used as collateral must be the principal residence of the borrower and meet certain property requirements.
  • The borrower must have sufficient equity in their home to qualify for the loan amount they need.
  • The property appraised value must be above the minimum Fannie Mae HomeReady loan minimum of 80% (Loan to Value) of the property market value or 100% of the home's sale price (the greater value), to avoid mortgage insurance (MIP) premiums.

Types of Proprietary Reverse Mortgages

Pennsylvania seniors can choose from different proprietary reverse mortgage products offered by lenders, including:

  • Jumbo Reverse Mortgages - Designed for high-value properties in Pennsylvania, these loans allow homeowners to access large sums of cash without depleting their equity or requiring a mortgage insurance policy.
  • Non-QM Reverse Mortgages - These reverse mortgages cater to Pennsylvania homeowners who do not meet the qualification standards for traditional government-backed reverse mortgage programs, such as those with recent income fluctuations or tax liens on their credit record.
  • Reverse Mortgage with a Line of Credit (HECM) - This product provides homeowners access to a credit line that can be drawn down as needed, and the loan repayments are only due upon sale or other event triggering payment. Interest charges only apply when you draw the credit line.

How Does it Work?

  1. The borrower or their designated representative submits an application to a lender offering the proprietary reverse mortgage product.
  2. The lender will require documentation, including proof of age, financial information, property ownership papers, property valuation report (appraisal), and the current condition of the property being offered as collateral.
  3. The lender will use this information to determine whether the borrower qualifies for the loan and calculate the loan amount.
  4. The lender arranges for a title search to verify the property details and any outstanding liens or debts recorded against the house.
  5. The property valuation will be completed by a certified appraiser to confirm the property value and loan amount.

Reverse Mortgage Benefits for Pennsylvania Seniors

  • Access to cash for home renovations or other expenses.
  • No monthly payments to make while in the home.
  • Borrower receives the total amount of the loan up-front.
  • Loan amount varies between 40% to 55% or more of the property appraised value or 100% of the property sale price, but not more than 70% to 85% of the total property value, the actual amount depending on your age, property type and the value of the property. This is called the Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio.

Things to Consider Before Choosing a Proprietary Reverse Mortgage

  • Evaluation of the loan terms to determine whether the benefit outweighs the possible drawbacks.
  • Understanding the repayment terms for the loan including the final payment conditions when selling the property.
  • Talking to a financial advisor or other professional for independent guidance on reverse mortgage options.

Consulting with a professional reverse mortgage expert in Pennsylvania or seeking expert advice is advisable before making any long-term financial commitment, especially when considering borrowing a sum of money using any type of proprietary reverse mortgage loan product.
