Tenure Payments: Guaranteed Income for Life

By Reverse Mortgage Guide Team Updated: April 12, 2024
𝕏 Share f Share in Share P Pin

Key Takeaways

  • A Tenure payment plan acts like an annuity, paying you every month.
  • The payments continue even if your loan balance exceeds the home's value.
  • The payments are tax-free because they are classified as loan advances.

For many seniors, unlocking the equity in their homes is a powerful way to secure financial stability in retirement. In this comprehensive guide, we'll break down the specific rules and regulations that govern reverse mortgages in your area.

For seniors who have run out of retirement savings and are trying to survive solely on a meager Social Security check, a lump sum of cash is often dangerous. It is too easy to spend it all in three years and end up broke again.

For these seniors, the FHA offers a payout option designed to permanently solve monthly cash flow crises: The Tenure Payment Plan.

What is a Tenure Payment?

A Tenure plan essentially turns your house into an annuity. Instead of giving you a lump sum, the reverse mortgage lender agrees to send you a fixed, guaranteed check on the first of every month.

Because the IRS classifies these payments as a loan advance rather than income, the check is 100% tax-free and does not affect your standard Social Security benefits.

The Guarantee of "Life"

The most extraordinary feature of the Tenure plan is the FHA guarantee.

The lender calculates your monthly payment amount based on your age and equity. Once that amount is locked in, the payments will continue for as long as you live in the home as your primary residence.

If you live to be 105 years old, and the total amount of money the lender has sent you vastly exceeds the actual market value of your house, the lender cannot stop the payments.

Because of the FHA insurance fund, you will continue receiving your $1,200 check every single month, even though your home equity was technically exhausted a decade ago.

The Drawback

Because the lender is taking on the massive risk that you might live to 105, the math they use to calculate your monthly check is very conservative.

If you have $200,000 in available equity, a Tenure payment might only offer you $1,100 a month. If you are 85 years old and dealing with terminal illness, choosing a Tenure plan is a terrible mathematical decision, as you will likely pass away long before extracting the full $200,000 of available equity.

Crucial Eligibility Factors

  • Age Requirement: You typically must be at least 62 years old to qualify.
  • Home Equity: You must own your home outright or have a significant amount of equity built up.
  • Property Taxes & Insurance: Borrowers are still entirely responsible for ongoing property taxes, homeowners insurance, and general maintenance.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens when I pass away?

When the last surviving borrower permanently leaves the home, the loan becomes due. Your heirs can choose to sell the home to repay the loan, or refinance the home if they wish to keep it.
