Proprietary Reverse Mortgage Guide for Kentucky Seniors

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Kentucky seniors may be eligible for a proprietary reverse mortgage, a type of home equity conversion loan designed to provide homeowners with tax-free funds.

A proprietary reverse mortgage is typically provided through a private lender, rather than a government-backed FHA Title program. This type of mortgage is not insured by the Federal Housing Administration (FHA) or the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD), but is instead backed by the lender.

To qualify for a proprietary reverse mortgage, borrowers typically must be at least 62 years old and own their home free and clear, or have a low balance on their existing mortgage. They must also occupy the home as their primary residence.

Lenders consider a variety of factors when determining whether to approve a borrower for a proprietary reverse mortgage, including:

  • Age: As reverse mortgage lenders are required to follow age requirements, borrowers must be 62 years old or older.
  • Home value: The home's value is critical to the amount the borrower may receive through a reverse mortgage.
  • Credit history: A good credit history may increase the likelihood of loan approval, but poor credit history may not necessarily prevent an individual from qualifying for a reverse mortgage loan.
  • Cash flow: A lender assesses a borrower's existing income and ongoing living expenses to determine whether the borrower can afford repayment of the reverse mortgage.
  • Health and life expectancy: Proprietary lenders use data from the U.S. Social Security Administration to estimate borrowers' life expectancy to determine loan eligibility and repayment options.

The proprietary reverse mortgage process typically involves the following steps:

  1. Initial consultation: The borrower meets with a qualified loan originator to discuss eligibility, terms of the loan, and any available loan options.
  2. Application and credit report: The borrower completes a loan application, provides financial documentation, and undergoes a credit review.
  3. Appraisal: A licensed appraiser determines the value of the borrower's home. This will be used to determine the home's maximum claim to equity, which could limit the amount the borrower may qualify for.
  4. Loan processing: The lender reviews the application and appraised value of the home to confirm the borrower's eligibility for a proprietary reverse mortgage.
  5. Loan closure: Once approved, the borrower signs the loan documents and the lender disburses the loan funds.
  6. Repayment terms: The borrower can choose to repay the loan when the property is sold, when the borrower moves away permanently, or when the borrower passes away. Alternatively, the borrower's heirs can pay off the loan in full or allow the lender to take possession of the property.

Kentucky seniors should note that while proprietary reverse mortgages offer access to tax-free cash, they require the borrower to meet strict qualification requirements and incur ongoing costs, including:

  • Upfront origination fee: The borrower pays a single fee, typically a percentage of the loan total.
  • Mortgage insurance: Proprietary reverse mortgages often come with non-recourse mortgage insurance premiums that add to the total cost.
  • Maintenance and property taxes: As with traditional mortgage loans, the borrower remains liable for property taxes and maintenance on their home.

When considering a proprietary reverse mortgage, Kentucky seniors should consult with a qualified and experienced loan originator to discuss available options and understand the potential risks and benefits associated with this loan product.
