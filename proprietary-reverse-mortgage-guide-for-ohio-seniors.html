Proprietary Reverse Mortgage Guide for Ohio Seniors

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Understanding Proprietary Reverse Mortgages for Ohio Seniors

A proprietary reverse mortgage is a type of home equity conversion mortgage (HECM) offered by private lenders rather than the Federal Housing Administration (FHA). While HECMs are the most well-known type of reverse mortgage, proprietary reverse mortgages can provide an attractive alternative for eligible Ohio seniors who want to access their home's equity without the need for monthly mortgage insurance premiums (MIPs).

How Proprietary Reverse Mortgages Work Proprietary reverse mortgages allow homeowners to borrow money using the equity in their home as collateral. In exchange, the lender receives a lien on the property, and the homeowner receives a lump sum, regular payments, or a line of credit. There are several proprietary reverse mortgage products available, including fixed-rate and variable-rate loans.

  • Lenders and Providers There are several lenders and providers that offer proprietary reverse mortgages in Ohio, including financial institutions, credit unions, and insurance companies.
  • Eligibility Criteria Proprietary reverse mortgage lenders typically require borrowers to meet certain eligibility criteria, such as being at least 62 years old, owning the home outright or having a low mortgage balance, and occupying the property as their primary residence.
  • Borrowing Limits In contrast to HECMs, which have strict borrowing limits based on FHA mortgage limits, proprietary reverse mortgages can offer higher loan amounts based on the homeowner's creditworthiness and the value of their home.
  • APR and Fees Proprietary reverse mortgage lenders may charge interest rates and fees that vary depending on the lender and the terms of the loan. Borrowers should carefully review the APR and fees associated with any proprietary reverse mortgage before signing an agreement.
  • Payment Plans and Options Borrowers can choose from various payment plans and options, including lump sums, regular payments, and lines of credit, to suit their financial needs and goals.

Benefits of Proprietary Reverse Mortgages for Ohio Seniors Proprietary reverse mortgages offer several benefits for Ohio seniors, including:

  • No MIPs Required Borrowers do not have to pay monthly MIPs, which can be a significant savings for those who opt for proprietary reverse mortgages.
  • Flexibility and Customization Proprietary reverse mortgage lenders can offer customized payment plans and loan terms based on the borrower's individual needs and circumstances.
  • No Income or Employment Restrictions Borrowers are not required to generate a certain income or employment level to qualify for a proprietary reverse mortgage.

Risks and Considerations While proprietary reverse mortgages can be an attractive option for some Ohio seniors, borrowers should carefully consider the following risks and considerations:

  • Accumulating Debt Borrowers should be aware that reverse mortgages can accumulate debt over time, which could lead to a decrease in the equity in their home.
  • Market Fluctuations and Interest Rates Changes in market conditions and interest rates can impact the value of the loan and the borrower's ability to repay it.
  • Lender Reputation and Qualifications Borrowers should choose a reputable and well-qualified lender to avoid potential risks and consequences.
  • Long-Term Consequences Borrowers should consider the long-term consequences of taking out a proprietary reverse mortgage, including how it may affect their estate plans, tax obligations, and financial well-being.

Ohio Resources for Seniors Ohio seniors considering a proprietary reverse mortgage should consult with a financial advisor, attorney, or counselor who specializes in reverse mortgage products and laws. Additionally, borrowers can contact the following resources for guidance and support:

  • Ohio Department of Insurance The Ohio Department of Insurance regulates and oversees reverse mortgage lenders in the state of Ohio.
  • Ohio Attorney General's Office The Ohio Attorney General's Office can provide guidance on how to identify and report potential scams and unscrupulous lenders.
  • Area Agencies on Aging (AAAs) AAAs offer a range of services and resources for seniors, including reverse mortgage counseling and education.
  • Counseling Services Many organizations offer free or low-cost counseling services to help seniors understand proprietary reverse mortgage options and make informed decisions.
