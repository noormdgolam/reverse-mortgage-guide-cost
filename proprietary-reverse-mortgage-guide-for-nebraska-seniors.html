Proprietary Reverse Mortgage Guide for Nebraska Seniors

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Understanding Proprietary Reverse Mortgages for Nebraska Seniors

As a senior citizen in Nebraska, you may be considering ways to access the equity in your home without having to sell or borrow from friends and family. One option you may want to explore is a proprietary reverse mortgage. This type of mortgage allows homeowners age 62 and older to borrow money using the equity in their home as collateral, with the opportunity to repay the loan when the property is sold or the borrower passes away.

A proprietary reverse mortgage is different from a Home Equity Conversion Mortgage (HECM), which is the most common type of reverse mortgage. The main difference is that private lenders, rather than the Federal Housing Administration (FHA), insure a proprietary reverse mortgage. This means that you may have more flexible eligibility requirements and a wider range of loan options available to you.

Benefits of Proprietary Reverse Mortgages

  • Access to Equity: A proprietary reverse mortgage allows you to tap into the equity in your home, giving you access to tax-free funds that can be used for living expenses, paying off debts, or financing home repairs.
  • No Monthly Payments: Unlike traditional mortgages, you won't have to make monthly mortgage payments, which can be beneficial for seniors living on a fixed income.
  • Flexibility: With a proprietary reverse mortgage, you can choose how you receive your funds, including a lump sum, monthly payments, or a line of credit.
  • No Age Restrictions: Unlike HECMs, proprietary reverse mortgages typically don't have age restrictions, making them a good option for younger seniors.

Risks and Considerations

While a proprietary reverse mortgage can provide financial benefits, there are risks and considerations to be aware of:

  • Accumulating Interest: Unlike traditional mortgages, the interest on a reverse mortgage accumulates over time, reducing the equity in your home.
  • Potential Loss of Ownership: If you fail to make payments on your property taxes or insurance, or if you pass away, the lender may foreclose on your property, resulting in a loss of ownership.
  • Complexity: Proprietary reverse mortgages can be complex, making it essential to work with a licensed mortgage professional who has experience with these types of loans.

Choosing a Lender

If you're considering a proprietary reverse mortgage, it's essential to research and compare rates and terms from multiple lenders. Look for lenders with a good reputation, competitive rates, and experienced mortgage professionals. You can also consult with a financial advisor or a non-profit organization specializing in housing and mortgage counseling for guidance and support.

Navigate the Reverse Mortgage Process

To get started with a proprietary reverse mortgage, follow these steps:

  1. Meet with a licensed mortgage professional who has experience with proprietary reverse mortgages.
  2. Discuss your financial situation, income, and expenses, as well as your goals for using the loan proceeds.
  3. Choose between a lump sum, monthly payments, or a line of credit for receiving your funds.
  4. Carefully review and sign the loan documents, making sure you understand the terms and conditions.
  5. Plan for ongoing property tax and insurance payments, as well as future interest accrual.
