Reverse Mortgage Guide

A reverse mortgage is a special type of home loan that allows homeowners, typically 62 years and older, to borrow money using their home's equity.

Reverse mortgages can provide a vital source of income, helping individuals meet everyday living expenses, consolidate debt, or make home repairs.

Understanding Types of Reverse Mortgages

There are three main types of reverse mortgages:

  • FHA-Insured Reverse Mortgage: Insured by the Federal Housing Administration (FHA), this is the most popular type, also known as the Home Equity Conversion Mortgage (HECM).
  • Proprietary Reverse Mortgage: These are non-government insured and typically require a lower upfront cost.
  • Jumbo Reverse Mortgage: Designed for high-value homes, these reverse mortgages have higher loan limits.

How Reverse Mortgages Work

A reverse mortgage is based on the home's value, current interest rates, and the borrower's age.

The borrower does not make monthly mortgage payments. The loan plus accrued interest is repaid when:

  • The homeowner sells the property
  • The homeowner passes away
  • The homeowner chooses to make repayments

Benefits of a Reverse Mortgage

Reverse mortgages offer several benefits, including:

  • No Monthly Mortgage Payments: Retain your monthly income and use it for other expenses
  • Increased Cash Flow: Borrow against your home's equity for various needs
  • Stay in Your Home: Preserve your home's ownership and independence
  • Long-Term Care Support: Use the funds for healthcare expenses or home care services

Risks and Considerations

It is essential to carefully consider the risks and potential drawbacks of reverse mortgages, including:

  • Accrued Interest: Interest may be added to the loan principal, increasing the debt
  • Fees and Charges: Closing costs, origination fees, and monthly servicing fees may apply
  • Property taxes, home insurance, and maintenance costs must be paid
  • Reduced Home Equity: As the loan increases, so does the amount borrowed and owed

Eligibility and Qualification

To qualify for a reverse mortgage, borrowers must meet specific requirements:

  • Age: Typically 62 years or older, but there are exceptions for certain circumstances
  • Home Value: A minimum value of the home must meet the lender's requirements
  • Credit Score: Borrowers typically must have a good credit history
  • Income: No income limits typically apply, but credit score impacts loan approval
  • Property Type: Primary residences are eligible, not vacation homes or investment properties.

Repayment Terms and Options

Careful consideration must be given to repayment terms.

The following options are available:

  • Settlement Option: Pay off the loan when you sell or move out
  • Term Option: Pay off the loan after a predetermined number of years
  • Line of Credit Option: Borrow a certain amount, repay, and re-borrow as needed
