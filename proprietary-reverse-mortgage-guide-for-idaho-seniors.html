Proprietary Reverse Mortgage Guide for Idaho Seniors

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Understanding Proprietary Reverse Mortgages

For Idaho seniors, proprietary reverse mortgages can provide a valuable source of tax-free funds, allowing homeowners to maintain a stable financial foundation in their retirement years.

A proprietary reverse mortgage is a type of non-recourse loan that allows homeowners aged 62 or older to borrow money using the equity in their primary residence. These loans are not insured by government agencies like the Federal Housing Administration (FHA) or the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA), but are instead offered by private lenders.

Key Features of Proprietary Reverse Mortgages

Idaho seniors should be aware of the following key features when considering a proprietary reverse mortgage:

  • Loan amounts: The amount available for borrowing is determined by the borrower's age, the value of the home, and current interest rates.
  • Interest rates: Loan interest rates are typically variable, and may be either adjustable or fixed.
  • Fees: Borrowers may be charged origination fees, servicing fees, and other costs associated with the loan.
  • Repayment options: Proprietary reverse mortgages allow borrowers to choose how they receive their loan proceeds, including taking a lump sum, setting up a line of credit, or arranging monthly payments.
  • No monthly mortgage payments: Borrowers are not required to make monthly mortgage payments, but are still responsible for paying property taxes and insurance.
  • Non-recourse loan: The loan is non-recourse, meaning that the borrower or their heirs will never owe more than the value of the home.

Types of Proprietary Reverse Mortgages

Borrowers in Idaho can choose from various proprietary reverse mortgage options, including:

  • Single-Purpose Reverse Mortgages: These loans are available from local non-profit organizations and allow borrowers to access limited funds for home repairs or renovations.
  • Flex Reverse, Genworth, Reverse Mortgage Plus: These are some of the most popular proprietary reverse mortgage programs in Idaho, offering more generous loan amounts and repayment options.
  • Home Equity Conversion Mortgage (HECM) Alternatives: These loans are designed for Idaho borrowers who are not eligible for a traditional HECM, often due to income or credit concerns.

Eligibility Criteria for Proprietary Reverse Mortgages

Idaho seniors who want to apply for a proprietary reverse mortgage must meet the following criteria:

  • Age: Borrowers must be at least 62 years old.
  • Occupancy: The primary residence must be occupied by the borrower.
  • Property value: The home's value must be sufficient to secure the loan.
  • Credit history: Borrowers must have an acceptable credit history.
  • There may be income limits or requirements for these loans.

Benefits and Risks of Proprietary Reverse Mortgages

While proprietary reverse mortgages can provide a valuable source of tax-free funds, there are also risks to consider, including:

  • Increased debt: Borrowers will still incur a mortgage debt, which may impact their financial security.
  • Fees and interest: Borrowers will be charged fees and interest, which may erode their equity over time.
  • Loan modification limitations: Borrowers may not be able to modify their loan in response to changing interest rates or financial circumstances.

It's essential for Idaho seniors to carefully weigh the benefits and risks of proprietary reverse mortgages before making a decision. They should consider consulting with a financial advisor or a reverse mortgage counselor to ensure they understand the terms and conditions of the loan.
