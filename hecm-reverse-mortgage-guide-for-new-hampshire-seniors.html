HECM Reverse Mortgage Guide for New Hampshire Seniors

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Understanding HECM Reverse Mortgages in New Hampshire A Home Equity Conversion Mortgage (HECM) reverse mortgage is a type of loan that allows seniors homeowners in New Hampshire to borrow money using the equity in their homes without making monthly mortgage payments. The loan is insured by the Federal Housing Administration (FHA) and can provide a steady income stream, a lump sum payment, or ongoing tax-free funds. New Hampshire's Department of Health and Human Services estimates that a significant portion of residents aged 65 and above live in their homes. To help seniors take advantage of the equity in their homes, New Hampshire offers various benefits, including assistance with energy-efficient home improvements and property tax deferral programs. ### Eligibility Requirements To qualify for a HECM reverse mortgage in New Hampshire, borrowers must meet the following requirements: - The property must be a primary residence in New Hampshire. - The borrower must be at least 62 years old. - The borrower must have a valid title to the property. - The borrower must be up to date on property taxes and insurance. - A creditworthy non-borrowing spouse may still live in the home. - The property must be maintained and used as the borrower's primary residence. ### Types of HECM Reverse Mortgage Benefits HECM reverse mortgages come in two primary forms: a fixed interest rate and a variable rate. - **Fixed Interest Rate**: This type of HECM reverse mortgage guarantees a fixed interest rate for the life of the loan, providing stable monthly disbursements or a lump sum payment. - **Variable Interest Rate**: This type of HECM reverse mortgage has a floating interest rate that increases and decreases according to market conditions. This may lead to changing monthly or lump sum payments. ### Pros and Cons of HECM Reverse Mortgages - **Advantages**: * Borrowers can use their home's equity without monthly mortgage payments. * Tax-free income stream for as long as the borrower lives. * Ability to access funds through a lump sum, monthly draws, or line of credit. * No income or debt-to-income verification is required to qualify. - **Disadvantages**: * Accumulating interest on a HECM reverse mortgage means lower principal balances. * The borrower must continue to pay property taxes and insurance. * Borrower heirs may need to pay off a HECM reverse mortgage from the estate balance. ### Steps to Obtain a HECM Reverse Mortgage in New Hampshire Obtaining a HECM reverse mortgage in New Hampshire requires careful planning and consultation with a licensed professional. Here is a general overview of the application process: 1. Contact a licensed reverse mortgage professional who is knowledgeable about HECM reverse mortgage requirements. 2. Gather financial and property documents, such as a current tax return, proof of income, and a deed or title report. 3. Complete the necessary paperwork and provide an appraisal of the property's value. 4. Submit the completed loan application for FHA review and approval. 5. Close the loan and sign the HECM reverse mortgage paperwork. ### New Hampshire Resources For comprehensive resources and guidance on senior housing options and assistance programs, seniors and prospective borrowers should contact: * New Hampshire's Department of Health and Human Services * New Hampshire Housing Finance Authority * Consumer Financial Protection Bureau In conclusion, a HECM reverse mortgage can be a viable solution for seniors in New Hampshire to access their home's equity without making monthly mortgage payments. Consult with a licensed reverse mortgage professional and explore available resources to understand the benefits and risks associated with a HECM reverse mortgage in New Hampshire's unique economic environment.
