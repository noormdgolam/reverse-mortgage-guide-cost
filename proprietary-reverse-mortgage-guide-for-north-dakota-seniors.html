Proprietary Reverse Mortgage Guide for North Dakota Seniors

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

North Dakota seniors are eligible to utilize proprietary reverse mortgages as part of their financial planning strategy. This type of mortgage is designed for homeowners aged 62 or older who reside in their primary residence, which can serve as collateral for the loan.

In comparison to government-backed reverse mortgages, proprietary reverse mortgages are offered by private lenders. These lenders do not require insurance from the US Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) and often have their own set of credit and property value requirements.

  • Some private companies that offer proprietary reverse mortgages, such as Longbridge Financial, may feature more lenient underwriting guidelines for borrowers with limited credit history.
  • However, this flexibility may come at a cost, as proprietary reverse mortgages often come with more stringent payment requirements compared to HUD-approved mortgages.

Private lenders may offer a diverse range of proprietary reverse mortgage products, designed to cater to various financial needs and objectives. For North Dakota seniors looking for a low-cost, non-recourse option to supplement their retirement income, a traditional proprietary reverse mortgage might be the most suitable choice.

An alternative for seniors seeking a more flexible borrowing option, or a loan with potentially lower upfront charges is the proprietary Home Equity Conversion Mortgage (HECM) loan alternative. A private lender's HECM Alternative, often called a proprietary Home Equity Loan (HEL), allows the borrower access to their home's equity via a line of credit or a lump-sum loan, minus the loan balance.

North Dakota seniors must carefully consider their financial situation and goals when determining whether a proprietary reverse mortgage is suitable for their situation. Borrowers have to weigh the benefits and risks involved, including fees associated with origination and servicing.

  • Private lenders may charge additional upfront costs for origination fees and appraisal fees compared to HUD-approved HECMs.
  • Holders of the HECM Alternative, may benefit from a lower loan balance, compared to HECMs due to higher property values often associated with North Dakota.

Additionally, North Dakota seniors should be aware of the following factors prior to pursuing a proprietary reverse mortgage:

  • Eligibility criteria: North Dakota seniors must be at least 62 years old, occupy the property as their primary residence and own the property free and clear, or have an existing mortgage on the property.
  • Loan approval process: This process involves a comprehensive review of the borrower's financial situation, and property value to determine the amount the borrower can borrow.
  • Loan repayment: Proprietary reverse mortgage borrowers will not need to repay the loan until the property is sold, transferred, or the mortgage holder passes away.

North Dakota seniors considering a proprietary reverse mortgage should reach out to a licensed mortgage professional or experienced financial advisor for personalized guidance and to ensure the product aligns with their financial circumstances.
