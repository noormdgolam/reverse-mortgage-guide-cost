HECM Reverse Mortgage Guide for Colorado Seniors

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

The Home Equity Conversion Mortgage (HECM) reverse mortgage is a type of government-insurance that allows senior homeowners in Colorado, aged 62 years or older, to borrow a portion of their home's equity without making regular mortgage payments.

A HECM loan is insured by the Federal Housing Administration (FHA), making it a secure and reliable financing option for eligible borrowers. The loan allows homeowners to remain in their residence, enjoy the benefits of tax-free cash, and continue to receive any future appreciation in their home's value, minus a set interest rate and applicable fees.

Eligibility for a HECM loan in Colorado requires meeting the following criteria:

  • At least one homeowner must be aged 62 years or older
  • The property must be considered the primary residence of the borrower
  • The property must be a one-unit property, including condominiums and townhouses
  • The property must be worth a minimum amount, set annually by the Federal Housing Administration (FHA)
  • Homeowners must be current on property taxes and insurance

To proceed with a HECM loan, borrowers must work with an FHA-approved lender that is licensed to conduct business in the state of Colorado. A loan officer will guide you through the application process, which typically involves the following steps:

  1. Home appraisal to determine the property's value
  2. Loan application and review of creditworthiness
  3. Completion of property insurance requirements
  4. Inspections of the property's condition and necessary repairs
  5. Final loan approval and disbursement of funds

Clients may access their loan proceeds in three different ways:

  • One lump sum payment
  • Monthly installments
  • Line of credit, which can be drawn upon as needed

HECM loans generally offer flexible repayment options and tax-free proceeds, however homeowners must be aware of the potential risks and implications associated with these types of loans.

Repayment is generally required when the last borrower passes away, sells the property, or moves out of the property permanently. In such cases, the lender will repay any outstanding loan balance using proceeds from the sale of the property and any insurance policy payout, minus applicable fees and interest. Borrowers may also continue to make mortgage payments to repay the loan early.

HECM's tax-free proceeds can be used for any purpose, including retirement expenses, medical costs, and personal financial goals. In Colorado, a HECM can be a helpful tool for seniors seeking to enhance their retirement income, reduce financial strain, or support their family and loved ones.

It's crucial for homeowners in Colorado to carefully consider their individual financial situation and options before seeking a HECM loan. To protect their interests and receive unbiased information, homeowners are encouraged to work with state-certified reverse mortgage professionals or financial advisors experienced in HECM lending.

Additionally, Colorado homeowners should be aware of state-specific rules and regulations that may affect their access to reverse mortgage funding.

A reverse mortgage may not be the right fit for every homeowner in Colorado, however for those eligible, these government-insured loans can provide valuable benefits and peace of mind in retirement.
