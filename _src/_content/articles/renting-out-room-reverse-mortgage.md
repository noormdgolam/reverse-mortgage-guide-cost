With inflation and the rising cost of living, many seniors look for creative ways to supplement their fixed incomes. If you have a large home and a reverse mortgage, you might consider taking in a renter or a boarder. 

Is this allowed under FHA rules, or will it cause the bank to foreclose?

## The Primary Residence Rule

The golden rule of a Home Equity Conversion Mortgage (HECM) is that the property must be your **primary residence**. You must live there for the majority of the year.

As long as you are living in the home, **you are absolutely allowed to rent out a spare bedroom to a boarder, a friend, or a college student.**

The FHA does not prohibit you from generating rental income, provided the arrangement does not change the fundamental nature of the property. You are still the primary occupant. 

## What You Cannot Do

While renting a room is fine, there are several rental scenarios that will trigger a default and cause the lender to call the loan Due and Payable:

1. **Renting the Entire House:** You cannot move into an apartment or a nursing home and rent out your entire house to a family. If you do this, the home is now an investment property, not your primary residence.
2. **Commercial Operations:** You cannot convert the home into a commercial bed-and-breakfast, a boarding house with dozens of tenants, or a commercial daycare facility that violates local residential zoning laws. 
3. **Multi-Family Homes:** If you used a reverse mortgage to buy a 2-to-4 unit property (like a duplex), you *must* live in one of the units. You can freely rent out the other units. If you move out of your primary unit, the loan defaults.

## Short-Term Rentals (Airbnb / VRBO)

The rules regarding short-term rentals (Airbnb) are slightly murky and rely heavily on local zoning laws. 

Generally, renting out a single bedroom occasionally on Airbnb while you are living in the home is acceptable. However, converting the home into a high-volume short-term rental operation can trigger violations of your local municipal codes or HOA rules. If you violate local zoning laws, you are technically in violation of your FHA mortgage covenants, which could lead to foreclosure. 

Always ensure any rental activity complies strictly with your local city ordinances.
