Reverse mortgages have a reputation for being easy to get because there are no monthly payments. In reality, the FHA underwriting guidelines are incredibly strict. Thousands of seniors are denied every year, often after they have already paid $600 for an appraisal.

Here are the five most common reasons a reverse mortgage application is rejected.

## 1. Not Enough Equity (Short to Close)
This kills more loans than anything else. You cannot borrow 100% of your home's value on a reverse mortgage; you can usually only borrow 40% to 55%, depending on your age.
If your home is worth $400,000, and you owe $250,000 on your current traditional mortgage, the math fails. The reverse mortgage might only offer you $200,000 in borrowing power, which isn't enough to pay off the old $250k loan. You are "short to close" and the loan is denied unless you can bring $50,000 in cash to the closing table.

## 2. Failing the Residual Income Test
If your Social Security check is tiny, and your county property taxes are massive, you will fail the Financial Assessment. The underwriter will require a Life Expectancy Set-Aside (LESA) to pay your taxes. If you don't have enough equity to fund the massive LESA, the loan is denied.

## 3. Severe Property Disrepair
The FHA appraiser is ruthless. If your house has a sinking foundation, a roof with gaping holes, or is filled with hoarding debris that constitutes a fire hazard, the FHA will deem the property "uninsurable." Unless you can afford to fix these major structural issues before closing, the loan is dead.

## 4. Title Issues and Liens
The reverse mortgage lender must be in the first lien position. If the title search reveals a massive IRS tax lien, a messy divorce decree claiming half the equity, or an unresolved contractor's mechanic's lien, the lender will halt the process until the legal mess is cleared up in court.

## 5. Not the Primary Residence
If the underwriter discovers through tax returns or utility bills that you actually spend 8 months a year living with your daughter in another state, they will deny the loan. FHA HECMs are strictly for primary residences.
