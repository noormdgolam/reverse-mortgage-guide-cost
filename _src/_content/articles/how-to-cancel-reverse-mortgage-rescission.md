A reverse mortgage is a massive financial commitment. It is not uncommon for seniors to get halfway through the process and get cold feet, or for a family member to suddenly offer to loan them the money instead.

If you have started the process, how easily can you back out?

## Canceling Before Closing

**You can walk away from a reverse mortgage application at any time prior to signing the final closing documents.** 

The lender cannot force you to close the loan. Furthermore, the lender cannot charge you for origination fees, title insurance, or FHA mortgage insurance if the loan does not close.

The only money you will lose is the out-of-pocket expenses you paid to third parties during the process. For example, if you paid $500 for the FHA appraisal and $125 for HUD counseling, those fees are non-refundable because the services were completed. 

## The 3-Day Right of Rescission

What happens if you sit at the closing table, sign the massive stack of legal documents, and wake up in a cold sweat the next morning regretting your decision?

Under the federal Truth in Lending Act (TILA), anyone taking out a mortgage on their primary residence is granted a **3-Day Right of Rescission**.

This means you have three full business days (excluding Sundays and federal holidays) after signing the closing documents to cancel the transaction entirely, with absolutely no penalty.

Because of this law, the lender will not actually distribute your cash on the day you sign. They hold the funds in escrow. Once the midnight deadline on the third business day passes, the loan officially "funds," and the money is wired to your bank account.

## How to Exercise the Right of Rescission

If you decide to cancel during that 3-day window, you cannot simply call your loan officer and leave a voicemail. 

You must notify the lender **in writing**. At closing, you will be handed a specific "Notice of Right to Cancel" form. You must sign this form, date it, and ensure it is delivered to the lender (via certified mail, fax, or hand delivery) before the midnight deadline. 

Once the three days have passed and the loan has funded, you can no longer cancel. To get out of the loan at that point, you would have to pay off the entire balance (including all the closing costs that were financed into the loan).
