Many seniors choose to retire in manufactured housing due to its affordability and low maintenance. If you own a manufactured home and want a reverse mortgage, the process is possible, but the FHA guidelines are incredibly strict.

Lenders refer to manufactured housing as "mobile homes," though the legal definition depends heavily on when and how the home was built.

## The Age of the Home

The most inflexible rule regards the manufacturing date. 
To qualify for a Home Equity Conversion Mortgage (HECM), **the manufactured home must have been built after June 15, 1976.** 

This date marks the implementation of the HUD Manufactured Home Construction and Safety Standards. If your home was built in 1974, it is entirely ineligible for any FHA-insured loan, no matter how many upgrades you have made. You must locate the HUD certification tags (usually a red metal plate bolted to the outside of the home) to prove the manufacturing date.

## Land Ownership vs. Leased Lots

The second massive hurdle is land ownership.

A reverse mortgage is a real estate loan. Therefore, **you must own the land underneath the manufactured home.** 

If your home is located in a mobile home park where you pay a monthly "lot fee" or "pad lease" to a park owner, you cannot get a reverse mortgage. You do not own the real estate; you only own the physical structure, which is considered personal property (like a car) rather than real estate.

## Foundation and Structural Modifications

To qualify for a reverse mortgage, the manufactured home must be **permanently affixed to a foundation** that meets FHA engineering standards. The axles and towing tongue must be permanently removed.

Furthermore, if you have built additions onto the home (like a massive wrap-around porch, a structural sunroom, or an attached garage), you must hire a licensed structural engineer to certify that the additions do not compromise the structural integrity of the original manufactured home frame. 

Because of these stringent requirements, many lenders simply refuse to underwrite manufactured homes, so you may need to shop around for a specialist broker.
