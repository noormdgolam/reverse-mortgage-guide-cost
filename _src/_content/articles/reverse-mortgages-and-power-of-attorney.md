When an elderly parent is diagnosed with severe Alzheimer's or dementia, the family often needs immediate cash to pay for memory care facilities or 24/7 in-home nursing. 

If the parent owns their home free and clear, the children often try to use a **Power of Attorney (POA)** to take out a reverse mortgage on the parent's behalf. 

This is highly regulated by the FHA to prevent elder financial abuse.

## The FHA Rules for Using a POA

You cannot simply use a generic POA printed off the internet. The lender's legal department will aggressively scrutinize the document before allowing the loan to proceed.

1. **It Must Be Durable:** A "Durable" Power of Attorney remains legally valid even if the principal (the parent) becomes mentally incapacitated. If the document is not durable, it became void the moment the parent lost mental capacity, rendering it useless for the mortgage transaction.
2. **Explicit Real Estate Powers:** The POA must explicitly grant the "Agent" (the child) the legal authority to encumber real estate with debt. If it only grants power over medical decisions or checking accounts, the lender will reject it.
3. **The Competency Requirement:** This is the biggest hurdle. The parent must have been mentally competent *on the exact date they signed the POA*. If the lender suspects the children forced a parent with advanced dementia to sign a POA yesterday just to get a loan today, the transaction will be flagged for fraud and denied.

## The Medical Exception

Because the senior lacks mental capacity, they obviously cannot comprehend the mandatory HUD counseling session. 

The FHA allows the Agent (the child holding the POA) to complete the HUD counseling session on the parent's behalf. 

Using a POA to secure a reverse mortgage is a tedious process. It will add several weeks to the underwriting timeline as the title company and the lender's attorneys verify the legality of the document. If you anticipate your parents needing memory care in the future, ensure their estate attorney drafts a bulletproof Durable POA while they are still in perfect cognitive health.
