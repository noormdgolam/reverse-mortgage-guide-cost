While the "Tenure" payment plan offers guaranteed income for life, the monthly checks are often quite small. 

What if you don't need income for life? What if you are 62 years old, recently laid off, and you just need enough cash to survive for five years until your full Social Security and pension kick in at age 67?

This specific financial bridge is exactly what the **Term Payment Plan** was designed to build.

## How Term Payments Work

With a Term plan, you specify the exact number of months you want to receive a check. The lender takes your available equity pool and mathematically amortizes it over that exact timeframe.

**Example Comparison:**
Assume you have $150,000 in available equity. 
- If you choose a **Tenure Plan** (for life), the lender might offer you **$850 a month**.
- If you choose a **Term Plan for 5 Years** (60 months), the lender might offer you **$2,800 a month**.

Because the lender knows exactly when their liability ends (at month 60), they can safely distribute massive amounts of cash to you very quickly.

## What Happens When the Term Ends?

This is the critical risk of a Term plan. When month 60 arrives, you receive your final $2,800 check. On month 61, the checks stop completely. Your available equity is totally exhausted.

However, **you are not evicted.** 

As long as you continue to pay your property taxes and homeowners insurance, you can continue living in the home rent-free for the rest of your life, even though the loan is maxed out and the monthly checks have stopped. 

Term payments are highly strategic. They are brilliant for delaying Social Security (allowing your SS benefits to grow by 8% a year until age 70) or for paying for expensive in-home nursing care for an ailing spouse who has a short life expectancy.
