Proprietary Reverse Mortgage Guide for Maryland Seniors

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Understanding Proprietary Reverse Mortgages for Maryland Seniors

Maryland seniors who are homeowners facing financial challenges due to the rising costs of living or decreasing social security checks may be eligible for a proprietary reverse mortgage. This loan option allows homeowners to access a portion of the equity accumulated in their primary residence.

A proprietary reverse mortgage, often referred to as a jumbo reverse mortgage, allows seniors to access the equity in their home without having to pay monthly mortgage payments. These mortgage products are offered by private lenders and can provide homeowners with the financial flexibility they need to pay off debt, cover living expenses, or make home improvements.

Key Benefits of Proprietary Reverse Mortgages in Maryland

  • Borrower Benefits: A proprietary reverse mortgage is available to homeowners who are at least 62 years old, occupy the property as their principal residence, and have a sufficient amount of equity.
  • No Monthly Payments: Homeowners do not have to make monthly mortgage payments as long as the property continues to serve as their primary residence, one of their spouses, or a disabled or low-income child.
  • No Income or Credit Requirements: Seniors do not need to meet income, credit, or employment requirements to qualify for a proprietary reverse mortgage.
  • No Closing Costs or Funding Fees: Homeowners may be eligible for a lender credit towards upfront closing costs and funding fees, which can reduce the overall out-of-pocket expense.

The proceeds from a proprietary reverse mortgage can be used for various purposes, including:

  • Home Improvements: Homeowners can use the proceeds to make much-needed home improvements or repair existing structures to prevent maintenance issues.
  • Debt Consolidation: Seniors can combine debt from multiple financial institutions into a single mortgage payment or use the proceeds to pay off high-interest loans or credit cards.
  • Healthcare and Living Expenses: The proceeds can be used to cover medical expenses, pay off outstanding medical bills, or supplement limited retirement income.

Common Proprietary Reverse Mortgage Options in Maryland

Three common proprietary reverse mortgage options available in Maryland include:

1. HomeSafe of America (HsA) Mortgage: HsA offers a proprietary reverse mortgage option through a partnership with a leading financial institution. The HomeSafe of America (HsA) Mortgage includes a non-recourse feature, allowing borrowers to avoid owing more on the mortgage than the value of the property.

2. Reverse Mortgage Funding: Reverse Mortgage Funding (RMF) is another prominent private lender offering proprietary reverse mortgage options. RMF's product offers a non-recourse feature, a 2% annual interest rate floor, and other protective features designed to safeguard borrowers' interests.

3. Generation Mortgage: Generation Mortgage is another private lender offering proprietary reverse mortgage options, including a non-recourse feature, competitive interest rates, and loan amounts up to $2.5 million.

Before Applying for a Proprietary Reverse Mortgage in Maryland

It's essential for seniors considering a proprietary reverse mortgage to:

  • Consult with a Financial Advisor: It's crucial to consult with a financial advisor to determine if a proprietary reverse mortgage aligns with your financial goals and strategy.
  • Review and Compare Options: Compare different proprietary reverse mortgage options from private lenders to find the best interest rate and terms for the borrower's individual situation.
  • Assess Property Value and Equity: Homeowners should have a clear understanding of their property's value and their accumulated equity to determine how much of the equity they can access through a proprietary reverse mortgage.
  • Understand Tax Implications: Borrowers should consult with a tax advisor to understand the tax implications of receiving a proprietary reverse mortgage.

By taking the time to research and understand your proprietary reverse mortgage options, you can make an informed decision and access the financial freedom you need to live comfortably in your Maryland home.

Disclosures and Disclaimer:

As with any reverse mortgage product, borrowers should be aware of the risks and implications associated with proprietary reverse mortgages, such as the potential for foreclosure or decreased property equity over time.
