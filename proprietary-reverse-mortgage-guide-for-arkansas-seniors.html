Understanding Proprietary Reverse Mortgages for Arkansas Seniors

A proprietary reverse mortgage is a type of home equity conversion mortgage (HECM) that is insured by a private mortgage insurer rather than the US Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD). These loans are offered by lenders that have been approved by the Federal Housing Administration (FHA) and allow homeowners who are age 62 or older to borrow money using the equity in their primary residence.

In Arkansas, as in the rest of the United States, proprietary reverse mortgages are designed to help senior homeowners manage their cash flow and access funds for various expenses, such as healthcare, home repairs, or living expenses. To qualify for a proprietary reverse mortgage in Arkansas, homeowners must meet specific requirements, including:

  • Being at least 62 years old
  • Own the property outright or have a low balance on the current mortgage
  • Use the property as their principal residence
  • Not be delinquent on any federal debt
  • Participate in a consumer information session provided by a HECM counselor before signing the loan

Homeowners who take out a proprietary reverse mortgage can receive funds in various ways, including:

  • Cash payments
  • A line of credit
  • A combination of cash and credit line
  • Mortgage payments suspended or reduced

The available funds are determined by the amount of equity in the home, interest rates, and the borrower's age. Homeowners should note that reverse mortgage proceeds are tax-free, but they may impact government benefits, such as Medicaid. Furthermore, homeowners should be aware of the potential risks associated with reverse mortgages, including:

  • A reduction in the value of their estate
  • A decrease in their credit score
  • A potential increase in property taxes or insurance
  • The possibility of foreclosure

To ensure a smooth and informed experience, it's crucial for Arkansas seniors to work with a licensed loan officer who is experienced in proprietary reverse mortgages. Homeowners should ask questions and carefully examine the terms and conditions of the loan before signing, as well as understanding their options and alternatives, such as:

  • Home equity loans or lines of credit
  • Home equity installment loans
  • HECMs

In Arkansas, senior homeowners may also want to explore additional resources and programs that can help them make informed decisions, such as:

  • The Arkansas Housing Development Authority (AHDA)
  • The Federal Trade Commission (FTC)
  • The National Council on Aging (NCOA)

By taking their time and doing their research, Arkansas seniors can navigate the proprietary reverse mortgage process with confidence and make informed decisions about their financial future.
