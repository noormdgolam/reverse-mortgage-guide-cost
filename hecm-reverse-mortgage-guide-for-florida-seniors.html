HECM Reverse Mortgage Guide for Florida Seniors

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

HECM Reverse Mortgage Requirements in Florida for Seniors

A HECM reverse mortgage is a type of mortgage designed for homeowners aged 62 and above, which allows them to withdraw a portion of the equity in their home without making payments. To be eligible for a HECM reverse mortgage, the property must be the borrower's primary residence and must be located in the United States. The home must be owned free and clear, or have a low outstanding mortgage balance.

Benefits of a HECM Reverse Mortgage

The benefits of a HECM reverse mortgage include:

  • Retirement income: A HECM reverse mortgage allows homeowners to access cash from the equity in their home without having to sell their property.
  • No monthly mortgage payments: Borrowers are not required to make monthly mortgage payments to the lender.
  • Guaranteed loan amount: The loan amount is guaranteed by the US government, which reduces the risk to the borrower.
  • No restriction on using funds: Borrowers are free to use the funds for any purpose they see fit.
  • Sustainability of the loan: If the borrower moves out of the home permanently, the reverse mortgage is due and must be repaid.

Eligibility Restrictions of HECM Reverse Mortgage

The eligibility restrictions of a HECM reverse mortgage include:

  • Age requirement: Borrowers must be at least 62 years of age and own the home outright or have a low outstanding mortgage balance.
  • Property requirements: The home must be a single-family home, a 2-4 unit property if the borrower is occupying one of the units, or a condominium unit.
  • Insurance requirements: The home must have a current HUD-approved mortgage insurance premium (MIP).
  • Debt-to-income ratio: Borrowers must have sufficient income to cover living expenses and other debts.

Costs Associated with HECM Reverse Mortgage in Florida

There are several costs associated with a HECM reverse mortgage, including:

  • Mortgage insurance premiums (MIPs): MIPs can be deducted from the loan amount, and they increase with the loan balance.
  • Origination fees: Origination fees can range from 2% to 5% of the loan amount.
  • Discount points: Discount points can be paid at closing to reduce the interest rate.
  • Appraisal fees: An appraisal is required to determine the value of the home.
  • Inspection fees: Inspections may be required to ensure the property meets certain standards.

Reverse Mortgage in Florida and Property Preservation

Borrowers with a HECM reverse mortgage are required to continue making payments, such as homeowners insurance, property taxes, and maintenance expenses, to preserve their home. Failure to pay these expenses can lead to foreclosure. Borrowers should work with a reputable lender and consider hiring a property preservation company to help manage these responsibilities.

Reversing a Reverse Mortgage

There are several ways to reverse a reverse mortgage:

  • Home sale: The borrower can sell the home to repay the reverse mortgage.
  • Repaying the loan: Borrowers can make voluntary repayments on the loan balance.
  • Home equity conversion mortgage (HECM) termination: The lender can terminate the HECM and require immediate repayment of the loan.
  • Home equity loan: Borrowers can take out a new mortgage to cover the remaining loan balance and then sell the home.
